# Employment At Will
> Draconian [[Capitalism\|Capitalist]] Legal concept in Employment & Labour Law where any employee is employed at the will of the employer and the employer does not need a reason to fire you or give you any notice either.

This law is widely called draconian since it influences the lives of all employees since all the power rests with the employers who do not even need to give their employees notice so they could get the time to search for another job. On the other hand employees are supposed to give notice to the employers when they resign by choice? A law that is delibrately biased towards those above the masses (employees) is draconian by definition.

### Exceptions to the Rule
The only time that the termination from employment is considered unjust is if the employee can prove in court that the company practices one of the following:
1. Discrimination based on gender, race, age, religion, disability or national origin
2. Retaliation i.e., punishing employees for reporting illegal activities
3. Public Policy Violations i.e., employees cannot be terminating for serving on jury duty or exercising rights such as voting or for refusing to commit a crime.
4. Breach of Contract that promises job security or that the company signed with the employee. Agreements signed with an [[Employee Union\|Employee Union]] or promises made in other documentation provided to the employee such as an [[Appointment Letter\|Appointment Letter]] or Employee Handbook or Company Policy.

Essentially to get any compensation for termination from employment such as [[Severance Pay\|Severance Pay]] or [[Gratuity\|Gratuity]] one has to prove that the employer's operations themselves are illegal! Even then the compensation granted by the court may be much lesser than what you asked for.

Employment at Will is infamously practiced in 49 out of 50 states in the USA with Montana being the only state in the USA which requires the employer to have a just cause for terminating an employee after the 12-month probabation period in accordance to the Wrongful Discharge from Employment Act also called as the WDEA.
