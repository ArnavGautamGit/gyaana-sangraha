# Nothing to Hide Fallacy
> The opinion of denying the need for [[Personal Online Privacy\|Personal Online Privacy]] by stating that they have nothing to hide.

It is one of the worst opinions to have in privacy space.
An Average person has enough cameras in their house that if all of them start streaming to the internet, their house will turn into a set of the Indian show "Bigg Boss" (inspired by the show "Bigg Brother") with the sheer number of cameras watching them and recording them at all times.

> [!cite] [[Edward Snowden\|Edward Snowden]] said it best
> "Choosing not to support the right to privacy because you have nothing to hide is like choosing not to support the freedom of speech because you have nothing to say"
> 
> ~ Snowden said in Hong Kong when he uncovered the [[National Security Agency (NSA)\|NSA]]'s [[PRISM Program\|PRISM Program]] in 2013.
