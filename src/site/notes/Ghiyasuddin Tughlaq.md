# Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq
> Founding ruler of the [[Tughlaq Dynasty\|Tughlaq Dynasty]] who became the 15th [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] by assassination of the last ruler of [[Khilji Dynasty\|Khilji Dynasty]] named [[Mubarak Shah Khilji\|Mubarak Shah Khilji]].

Born under the name Ghāzi Malik, he took on the Regnal Name "Ghiyas ud-Din Tughlaq" which is also spelled as "Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq".

### Military Campaign to reintegrate Bengal
He spent a big chunk of his reign handling the re-merger of [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] back into Delhi after 3-4 generations of independent rule. During this time, Delhi was being managed by his son, [[Mohammad bin Tughlaq\|Jauna Khan]].

### Death & Succession
He was asssassinated by his own son [[Mohammad bin Tughlaq\|Jauna Khan]] when during the celebrations of Ghiyas's return after a successful Bengal campaign, Jauna wanted to present his father with a gift of elephants but in doing so he brought elephants on the same stage (dias) where his father (Ghiyasuddin) was standing. Ghiyasuddin died when the dias collapsed from the weight of the elephants when Ghiyasuddin stepped forth to inspect the elephants from up close and one of the elephants apparently crushed Ghiyasuddin's body in the fall after the dias collapsed.

It is unclear whether it was Jauna's genuine foolishness, a staged assassination masqueraded as Jauna's foolishness or if it was the carpenter (most artisans were hindu) who decided to leave structural weaknesses so this accident could take place. I personally believe that there is a high chance of the assassination to be staged since Jauna somehow did not die despite being on the same dias & the dias did not collapse until Ghiyasuddin came close to the elephants.

There is no way to know this truth is because the court proceedings & biographers will only write what Jauna likes since Jauna Khan himself became the 16th [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] under the regnal name [[Mohammad bin Tughlaq\|Mohammad bin Tughlaq]].
