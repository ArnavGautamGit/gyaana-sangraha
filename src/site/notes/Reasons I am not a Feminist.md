# Reasons why I am not a Feminist

I have not heard of women going closer to the frontlines than the artillery division and any frontline units are not utilised due to the fear of female soldiers being captured.

I have never heard a single feminist arguing in FAVOUR of male models being paid the same as female models in lines of work like modelling where women are objectively paid higher than men? If the objective is a gender-neutral, egalitarian society then why do they address all the issues only from the lens of women? Why do they not see male issues? Why do they downplay the effects of [[Misandry\|Misandry]]? Why is there so much talk about Masculinity & Male Privilege but no talk about [[Female Privilege\|Female Privilege]]?

Why does Feminism only selectively asks for representation for women to become CEOs, CFOs and similar high-paying white collar designations? Do feminists want men to be relegated to blue-collar work while they take all the high-paying ones? If yes, then how is it different from Sexism of the old days? Why do you want to reshape masculinity with new social constructs about manspreading and mansplaining instead of fixing yourself? Why does feminism talk more about men than it does about women? Why does it look like Feminism is rather trying to establish a matriarchy? Why should men support a Matriarchal system when women did not support Patriarchy?

If feminists were to justify this with some kind of payback for Patriarchy, it is effectively trying for two wrongs to make a right and brings us to the same question of why do they want men to support it then? If women want to be empowered, why don't they do something on their own? Why do want power to be handed to you on a platter just because "look how oppressed i was"?

### Credit Where it is Due
Despite all the reasons why I am not a feminst, I can genuinely understand why many identify as one. Despite making the personal lives and bedrooms of the couple into political battlegrounds I can clearly see how [[First-Wave Feminism (1848-1950s)\|First-Wave Feminism (1848-1950s)]] brought the ideas of Universal Right to Vote (known as the [[Universal Adult Franchise\|Universal Adult Franchise]] in India) among property rights and certain laws while the second wave of Feminism brought laws for women's safety against crimes like Domestic Violence, Rape, Molestation among other heinous things that I do not even wish to imagine - let alone be able to even comprehend what it would feel like for the women who were victims of such crimes.

I hope anyone reading this is able to understand that while I am not against what Feminism stood for, but I would rather support a purely egalitarian movement rather than supporting a selectively egalitarian movement which sees the world's inequalities ONLY from the woman's perspective and frames them into a victimised mindset of creating war against men.
