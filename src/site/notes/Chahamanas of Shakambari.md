# Chahamanas of Shakambari (शाकम्बरी चाहमाण/चौहाण)
> Also later called "Chauhans of Sambhar", they were a royal family belonging to the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajput Clan]] who ruled over the region of "Shakambari" with their capital in Ajmer. 
 
Shakambari and Sapadalaksha are Sanskrit names for the region surrounding Ajmer city which is now known as Sambhar in Hindi since the time of the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Empire]]. 
Although other branches of [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]] may have existed (And I will add to them) but from this specific line or branch, [[Durlabharaja I Chahamana\|Durlabharaja I Chahamana]] played a big role in the First War of the [[Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj\|Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj]] as the general of the [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratiharas]].

The most famous ruler from their dynasty is [[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] who unfortunately died in the [[Second Battle of Tarain (1192)\|Second Battle of Tarain (1192)]] due to certain events after [[First Battle of Tarain (1191)\|First Battle of Tarain (1191)]] and ended up as the last significant king from this main line of the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]].

### Other Branches originating from Ajmer
[[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] had a child named [[Govindaraja IV Chauhan\|Govindaraja IV Chauhan]] who was a minor during the war (probably under the age of 12 or 11) who had agreed for the [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Ajmer Chauhans]] to be the vassal of the Delhi Sultanate until Prithviraja's brother [[Hari Rāma Chauhan\|Hari Rāma Chauhan]] came to dethrone him and lead a revolution against the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]]. It can be assumed that Hari told his nephew to flee to Ranastambhapura (now called "Ranthambore") and showcase to the Sultanate how he is loyal to the throne so that his life can be spared. This assumption is attested by the fact that when the revolution failed Hari was easily granted asylum by his nephew ruling Ranthambore.

[[Govindaraja IV Chauhan\|Govindaraja IV Chauhan]] founded the [[Chahamanas of Ranastambhapura\|Chahamanas of Ranastambhapura]] branch of the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]] which went on till [[Siege of Ranstambhapura (1301)\|Siege of Ranstambhapura (1301)]] carried out by the Sultan of Delhi, [[Alauddin Khilji\|Alauddin Khilji]].

Some time in 11th century, a new branch at [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Jalor]] (1160-1311) came to be from the smaller branch of the Chahamanas at [[Chahamanas of Naddula\|Naddula]] (950-1197) which itself came from the Ajmer mainline. 

While the Naddula branch was loyal to the mainline in [[Second Battle of Tarain (1192)\|Second Battle of Tarain (1192)]] and later vassalised themselves with the [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] and mostly ended in the [[Battle of Kasahrada (1197)\|Battle of Kasahrada (1197)]] with very few survivors (who survived by either agreeing to be vassals or survived by seeking asylum in Jalor).

The branch of [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Chahamanas of Jalor]] ruled for much longer since it had agreed to become a vassal of the new [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] fairly soon in the 1190s without subsequent war but later went independent when weak rulers were in power and Jalor was ruled by [[Udaysimha\|Udaysimha]]. 

After the Delhi Sultanate had captured the region of [[Chahamanas of Naddula\|Chahamanas of Naddula]] after winning in the [[Battle of Kasahrada (1197)\|Battle of Kasahrada (1197)]], the Jalor Chauhans recaptured it sometime during the reign of Jalor King [[Udaysimha\|Udaysimha]] under the nose of Delhi's Sultan [[Aram Shah\|Aram Shah]].

Eventually [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Chahamanas of Jalor]] were taken out by [[Alauddin Khilji\|Alauddin Khilji]] in the [[Siege of Jalor (1311)\|Siege of Jalor (1311)]].
