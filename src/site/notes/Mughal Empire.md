# Mughal Empire
> An Empire in Northern India ruled by a singular dynasty of rulers who had Turko-Mongol lineage from [[Timur\|Timur]] and [[Genghis Khan\|Genghis Khan]]

Mughals were the first ones to introduce [[Cannon Artillery\|Cannon Artillery]] and [[Early Firearms\|Early Firearms]] in India after establishing their empire by winning the [[First Battle of Panipat (1526)\|First Battle of Panipat (1526)]] against [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] of the [[Lodhi Dynasty\|Lodhi Dynasty]].

Although the term Sultanat/Saltanat is sometimes seen to be used for them as well in contemporary [[Literary Records\|Literary Sources]] and vernacular literature, it was a misnomer spawned solely from the habit of calling the region under Delhi as [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]].

To distinguish themselves as a separate power, [[Babur\|Babur]], the founder of the Mughal Empire used the newely founded city of Agra as their capital & changed their title to "Bādshāh" which was the Arabic version of the Farsi/Persian term "Pādshāh" meaning Emperor i.e., they do not rule in anyone else's name but their own. Briefly, [[Razia Sultān\|Razia Sultān]] of [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] had also used the title of Pādshāh.
