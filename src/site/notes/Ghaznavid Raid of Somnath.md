# Ghaznavid Raid of Somnath
In 1024 AD, 6 years after his raids of [[Ghaznavid Raid of Mathura\|Mathura]] and [[Ghaznavid Raid of Kannauj\|Kannauj]], [[Mahmud of Ghazni\|Mahmud of Ghazni]] decided to raid the [[Somnath Jyotirlinga\|Somnath Jyotirlinga]] in present-day Gujarat which was controlled by the .

### Backdrop of the Raid
It is unclear how [[Mahmud of Ghazni\|Mahmud of Ghazni]] reached Somnath despite not having a direct way to get there, it is assumed he moved from Sindh into the territory of [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] ruling by the popular name of "[[Solanki Dynasty\|Solanki Dynasty]]" there.

These were the times of [[Bhimadeva I\|Bhimadeva I]] who ruled from Anahilapataka (present-day Patan) just like the Solanki kings before him and even the [[Chavda Dynasty\|Chavda Dynasty]] before the Solankis ruled from the city of Patan. It was quite far away from the Somnath Temple.

### Mahmud's Plan
[[Mahmud of Ghazni\|Mahmud of Ghazni]] decided that raiding Somnath in a surprise raid will be the most effective option, especially when the army moves in the night right before sunrise so visibility will be low and clouds of dust caused by the movement of cavalry would not be very easily visible from afar.

### The Three Day Battle for the Somnath Jyotirlinga
In 1025-1026 AD, Ghazni arrives at Somnath after ransacking many other temples on the way.
Many [[Hinduism\|Hindus]] had built ramparts and fortifications around the temple to protect it because Ghazni had developed a reputation as a raider now, many civilians had decided to protect the city themselves in case a surprise raid happens. And the night of the attack, Brahmins and devotees of [[Lord Shiva\|Lord Shiva]] fought with a handful of soldiers to deter the Ghaznavid raid.

By evening of the first day, Mahmud's forces stood on the walls and ramparts around the Somnath Temple and it since it was the time of the [[Jummah Namaz\|Jummah Namaz]], they called everyone for prayer. Meanwhile the Hindus retreated to the Sanctum Sanctorum inside the temple praying to the Shivalinga.

As the sun announced the morning, Hindus banded together for a massive counterattack! From inside the temple, Hindus bravely banded together armed with whatever they could find from pitchforks and sticks all the way to swords picked from fallen soldiers they struck back with all their might and reclaimed the ramparts by the end of the second day.

On the third day of the war, the Ghaznavid forces attacked and the lack of military strength became the difference as the massive casualties with civilians neither trained in war nor medicine to heal the wounded finally began to take its toll and the devotees were pushed to the gates of the shrine where in the final melee the untrained wounded Hindus were finally defeated, those severely wounded along with women and children that tried to flee on boats were shot by the archers or drowned due to exhaustion.

### Mehmud's show of degeneracy
Mahmud of Ghazni entered the temple with cows. Those cows were then culled in front of the shrine and a garland of cow meat and beef was hung it around the Shivlinga by Mahmud himself before he smashed the revered Shivlinga (that too one of the 12 original Jyotirlinga) to pieces and taking the pieces to Ghazni, where the pieces were added to the steps of the Jami Mosque (Jami Masjid) in the city of Ghazni, the capital of his [[Ghaznavid Empire\|Ghaznavid Empire]].
