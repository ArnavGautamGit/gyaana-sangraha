### How does Monero work?
Most Cryptocurrencies like [[Bitcoin (BTC)\|Bitcoin (BTC)]], [[Ethereum (ETH)\|Ethereum (ETH)]] and others like them are hosted on a public [[Blockchain\|Blockchain]] where all transactions along with sender, receiver and transaction amount is logged & hence movement can be studied. 

Monero (unlike puclic crypto) is NOT searchable and DOES NOT have a permanent record of transaction due to it not having a [[Public Ledger\|Public Ledger]] that lists all transactions as they are... 

Monero uses [[Ring Signatures\|Ring Signatures]] which involve sending a group of sender IDs, receiver IDs and transaction amounts (e.g. random 10 values of each of them) while the real values could be any one of them at random. Although now they are going to switch to [[FCMP++\|FCMP++]] and move away from [[Ring Signatures\|Ring Signatures]] in general to protect the privacy of users much better.

Every time one receives a payment via Monero, there is a new one-time payment address that is created to replace the last one used, these are called "[[Stealth Addresses\|Stealth Addresses]]".

### Why is it attracting bans & removals?

For the general public it is amazing privacy but in the wrong hands of the rich it is a tool of money laundering. This is the excuse by which governments are banning Monero & these bans are why Monero is also attracting bans and removals from [[Centralised Crypto Exchanges\|Centralised Crypto Exchanges]] but Monero can be directly bought in a [[Decentralised Crypto Exchange\|Decentralised Crypto Exchange]] - for now.

Due to its nature of being privacy-respecting one would want to only use Monero quite anonymously, however the only way to trace monero transactions is to trace the act of purchasing monero since other [[Cryptocurrency\|Crypto]] that uses a [[Public Ledger\|Public Ledger]] are trackable i.e., any agency tracking you will know how much monero you traded, even if they do not know where you sent it.

The only option is to avoid them being able to detect your exact identity, which is why it is recommended to use [[The Onion Router (TOR)\|The Onion Router (TOR)]] alongside a [[Virtual Private Network (VPN)\|Virtual Private Network (VPN)]] to completely be able to spoof your identity. You could also use a [[Virtual Machine\|Virtual Machine]] which has TOR inside it while your main machine connects to a Wifi with a VPN only...

### How to mine for Monero?
Mining for Monero can be done in other ways than the method described here but the most common method is to download a program known as "XMRig" and then run its configuration wizard, one can find many videos on this including some by Indian creators like this particular tutorial by [Honey With Crypto](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=InRr2iUpvRE).

In the wizard, you will be asked to add a wallet address (where the mining rig will deposit all your Monero) and then also in the same page ask you for your location. As of May 2026, there is only 1 location option for "Asia".

### Op-Sec for best Privacy & Security in 2026

Monero provides quite a bit of [[Personal Online Privacy\|Personal Privacy]] and in the Finance Sector, apart from Privacy Advocates, the other people that need Financial Privacy are running Money Laundering Schemes or some other Financial Scams. Monero was often demanded by [[Black-Hat Hackers\|Hackers]] who were employing [[Ransomware\|Ransomware]] Attacks.
