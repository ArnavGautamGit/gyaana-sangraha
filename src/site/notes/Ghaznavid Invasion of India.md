# Ghaznavid Invasion of India
In the year 1001, the [[Ghaznavid Empire\|Ghaznavid Empire]] attacked and captured Peshawar, soon cities like Multan and others started to fall. This was because [[Hindu Shahi Dynasty\|Hindu Shahi Dynasty]], and a small principality in Multan could not could not form a singular front and were rather dispersed, unlike the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]]. 

Although Ghazni never established permanent control over much of present-day India due to the resistance of local rulers and especially the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]] of whom the [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandelas]] sent [[Mahmud of Ghazni\|Mahmud of Ghazni]] some money to effectively shoo him away with what was pennies to them (due to the sheer revenue [[Khajuraho Group of Temples\|Khajuraho Group of Temples]] was generating).

Using Peshwar and Multan as their permanent bases after defeating the Hindu Shahis [[Mahmud of Ghazni\|Mahmud of Ghazni]] and the [[Ghaznavid Empire\|Ghaznavid Empire]] raided multiple locations.
He also named his general as [[Mohammed Qasim Hindu Shah (Firishta)\|Mohammed Qasim Hindu Shah (Firishta)]], which losely translates to "Mohammed Qasim, the lord of Hindus (or Hindu Land i.e., "al-Hind" in Arabic)" 

Mahmud picked the richest city of the country (Mathura), the most historic capital (Kannauj/Kanyakubja) and the most spiritually sacred (Somnath) to be raided and looted.
