# Legal Warrant
> Legal Document issued by a judge of [[Indian Judiciary#Judicial Courts in India\|Indian Courts]] that allows the Government to search a person's property or arrest the person against whom the warrant is lodged.

It is a necessary permission slip that must be signed by a judge in the Court of Law so that actions that largely meddle with a person's date to day life (such as searching the property and arresting a person from the family) can be regulated by the Judiciary acting as a neutral third party which is bound to listen to the case before it decides whether there is merit in the argument presented by the executive for the issuiance of the warrant.

The final decisive power of whether the executive can actually exercise this right is granted by the judge by choosing to either: issue the warrant (often by signing it) or denying the request. 

### Why are Warrants required?
The executive arm of democracy (and by extension the legislature commanding it) cannot be allowed to strut around searching any random person's property like cars and homes and "keep searching until something incriminating is found" else it will completely remove the faith of their being any due process and system of government. 

A multitude of problems will arrive if the mindset of "people for themselves" or the notion that the "government works for itself and threatens the freedom of the people" catches on with an armed rebellion being the largest and the most devastating one on the people, the nation/state, its military and its economy. 

### Loopholes in the System
In many jurisdictions, Search Warrants are not needed for searching a database and any of the "personal data" of the person is not considered the person's property regardless of whatever database it is on (a separate one or the govt's own database). In such jurisdictions (like the USA) search warrants are not needed for digital data stored on a database.

This means that if the [[Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)\|Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)]] were to create their own database on all citizens of the USA and other countries then pressing the search button on a digital database is not a "search" in legal terms. And database data is often acquired via purchase or making [[Subpeona Requests\|Subpeona Requests]]. Although people argue that it is against the [[4th Ammendment of the Constitution of the USA\|4th Ammendment]] but the founding fathers of the USA (or any country) cannot foresee what technology could exist 250 years later and the loopholes people may try to find.
We can learn more about this in: [[Third Party Doctrine of Digital Privacy\|Third Party Doctrine of Digital Privacy]]. It is important to know the basics of the law of the USA as well since most services are based there.

Secondly, in many jurisdictions, the person against whom a search warrant is issued is not heard. Since only the government's version of the story is heard in court people also feel that it is going to be one-sided. The executive should only be able to ask for a warrant on either a hearing or via a notice which the person subject to a probable warrant be able to argue against via a similar notice or a hearing.
