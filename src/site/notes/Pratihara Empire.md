# Pratihara Empire
> Empire under the [[Pratihara Rajputs\|Pratihara Clan of Rājpūts]] who captured the Gurjaradesha (northern & eastern Gujarat) which was under the [[Gurjara (गुर्जर)\|Gurjara (गुर्जर)]].

Although I have not researched enough to check whether the mainline rulers such as [[Nagabhata I\|Nagabhata I]], [[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]] and [[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] were distinct from [[Pratiharas of Jodhpur\|Pratiharas of Jodhpur]] or not, it can be assumed that they are somehow related.

The Pratihara Empire was also one of empires involved in the [[Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj\|Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj]] and they ended up winning the conflict, despite being on the verge of destruction in 799 AD.

### Noble Families
The Empire was made of many noble families who were either smaller chieftains at the time who would rule independently or were already rulers of sizable swaths of land.

Here is a list of certain families that were already kings and decorated generals:
1. [[Guhilas of Medapata\|Guhilas of Medapata]] eventually were known as the "Guhilas of Mewar"
2. [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] were the military generals of [[Chavda Dynasty\|Chavda Dynasty]] in Gujarat.
3. [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chahamanas of Shakambari]] eventually were known as the "Chauhans of Sambhar", sometimes also referred to as the "Chauhans of Ajmer"
4. [[Chavda Dynasty\|Chavda Dynasty]] were the rulers of Gujarat and some portions of Maharashtra & south Rajasthan who established the city of Anahilvada (now "Patan") and made it their capital until they were overthrown by the [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] to form the [[Solanki Dynasty\|Solanki Dynasty]]. 

Many other families eventually got rewarded for their bravery and were rewarded with control over territory, these include:
1. [[Parmar Rajputs\|Parmar Rajputs]]
2. [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandela Rajputs]]
3. other families

### Rājputānā History after Tripartite Wars (833 AD to 910 AD)
After the death of [[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] in 833 AD, the empire went to Ramabhadra. He was already quite old by the time he got to the throne and experienced many internal difficulties during his reign. Ramabhadra passed away after just 3 years into his reign, we do not know whether he was assassinated or whether he died of natural causes. Ramabhadra's reign is only documented in ONE literary source which is "Prabhavakacharita" by a [[Jaina Philosophy (Jainism)\|Jain]] author 

Ramabhadra's son [[Mihira Bhoja\|Mihira Bhoja]] became the king in 836 AD and received a crumling empire which Mihira did not just prevent from collapse, but he was powerful enough to turn it around and build it to a great empire that spread from the river Narmada in MP & Gujarat to the river Sutlej in Punjab. In the east, the empire under him was expanded all the wayfrom Gujarat to Bengal. He took on the title "Ādivarāha" as a bhakt (devotee) of [[Lord Vishnu\|Lord Vishnu]] and minted coins with the same title of "Ādivarāha" inscribed to position himself as just a vessel for Lord Vishnu's divine play (also asserting his [[Divine Right to Rule\|Divine Right to Rule]]).

[[Mihira Bhoja\|Mihira Bhoja]] defended India from the Arabs by repeatedly defeating the [[Abbasid Caliphate\|Abbasid Caliphate]] in the battlegrounds of Sindh. The Persian text Hudud-ul-Alam states that taking Sindh is impossible as long as the "Rai of Qinnauj" (i.e., Raja of Kannauj) sat on the throne whose massive army had 150,000 cavalry and 800 war elephants (we know they are talking about Mihira Bhoja since Kannauj was the Pratihara Capital)

Mihira Bhoja's reign came to an end when he passed away in 885 AD and was succeeded by [[Mahendrapāla I\|Mahendrapāla I]] (not to be confused with another ruler of the same name in [[Pala Dynasty of Bengal\|Pāla Dynasty]]). Despite being the son of such an empire-builder as Mihira Bhoja, Mahendra was not considered someone to be feared and lost some territory in Punjab to a Kashmiri King [[Sahkaravarman\|Sahkaravarman]] but Mahendrapāla was least bothered due to being more concerned with the Arabian Abassid Caliphate.

After Mahendrapāla's reign from 885 to 910 is the time when they started to have surnames with the Pāla suffix and Wikipedia incorrectly adds them to the Pāla dynasty for this reason. This is also the time when [[Khajuraho Group of Temples\|Khajuraho Group of Temples]] was made by the [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandela Rajputs]] who were the vassals of the Pratihara. This was the time during which the "Vesara" form of [[Hindu Temple Architecture\|Hindu Temple Architecture]] was created, mastered and popularised by the Pratiharas and Chandelas.

### Cultural Contributions
[[Khajuraho Group of Temples\|Khajuraho Group of Temples]]
"Vesara" style of [[Hindu Temple Architecture\|Hindu Temple Architecture]].
Defending against [[Islamic Colonialism\|Islamic Colonialism]] by defending against many Muslim invaders.

### Eventual Decline and Reformation (910 AD to 1036 AD)
Succession troubles after 910 AD became more eminant while later rulers became complacent and clans like the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]] and [[Parmar Rajputs\|Parmar Rajputs]] gained precedence among all the other feudatories, the overthrow of the [[Chavda Dynasty\|Chavda Dynasty]] to form the [[Solanki Dynasty\|Solanki Dynasty]] in Gujarat by the [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] cemented the fact that feudatories were quite independent and the Pratiharas slowly shrunk to a small region in the Ganga-Yamuna Doab as the Rajputs began behaving like a confederates of Independent States. This is how the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]] was slowly born.
