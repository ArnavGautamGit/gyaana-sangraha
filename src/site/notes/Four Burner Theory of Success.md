# Four Burner Theory of Success
> A theory which draws an analogy of a human being like a cooking stove with 4 burners strapped to a limited fuel which is the equivalent to energy or motivation or whatever else.

The Theory was first given by [[James Clear\|James Clear]], the very author who sometime later (after giving this theory in his earlier writings) then wrote the book [[Atomic Habits\|Atomic Habits]].

### My Customisation to the Theory
Although, the actual theory defines the 4 burners as Work, Health, Family and Friends; personally I prefer renaming them to Work, Health, Relationships and Hobbies. Since you cannot focus just on Family without going on trips and end up putting some focus or energy on friends, I often put both family & friends in one burner with the last burner taken up by hobbies instead. I would encourage readers to similarly customise the theory to their needs.

### Basic Idea behind the Theory
Imagine yourselves as a cooking stove with 4 burners which are Work, Relationships, Health and Hobbies. You have limited fuel but if all 4 burners will go full power, you will exhaust all the fuel really rapidly leaving you burnt out but if you can regulate the burners, turning few of them down or completely off while others go full power, you can regulate your energy flow better.

### Strategies to Have it All
1. Settings boundaries & communicating them to everyone else
2. Having [[Personal Online Privacy\|Personal Privacy]] and not being always so easily reachable. 
3. Using separate devices for Work.
4. Incoroporate relationships by having the same friends also be your workout buddies and try to keep multiple burners on low burn simultaneously.
5. If you have kids, plan a vacation or staycation with other friends who also have kids.
