# My Speech is Algorithmic
> One of the notes on my series of Self-Awareness where I discover and document some things about myself.

Different people have a different way of talking, I have realised that I started using Obsidian because it suited the style of expression and learning that comes naturally to me.

The realisation hit me while I was talking to a friend about why I have different accents when talking in different languages and why my voice drops or picks accents when talking to different people even if it is the same language. While talking, I naturally began talking about the different languages I know, how their accents sound, then their formality and finally prividing a closing argument to explain my speech-related mannerisms.

During this conversation I realised how topics moved like Nested [[Functions in Programming\|Functions]], i.e., if the topic we are talking about needs a term or sub-topic to be explained then I will explain that subtopic first before returning to the main topic. This has its own advantages and disadvantages since I am able to fully explain any topic before we move on with the conversation ahead, it is also to be noted that this can make my argument longer and it may sound like disconnected if I am interrupted or not given enough time to completely bring the conversation back to the close.

### Tips for myself
1. Only talk to knowledgeable people who already have a basic understanding of the subject; it reduces the number of smaller "nested functions" (sub-topics) that need to be explained.
2. Because I am able to completely explain things in depth due to these reasons, I can easily hold conversations for long & explain concepts well. This makes me a good teacher.
