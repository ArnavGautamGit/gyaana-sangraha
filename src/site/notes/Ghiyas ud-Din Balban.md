# Ghiyas ud-Din Balban
> The 9th and also the last major [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] from the [[Mamluk Dynasty (Delhi)\|Mamluk Dynasty]].

![[Ghiyas ud-Din Balban.png\|Ghiyas ud-Din Balban.png]]

He came into public light and affairs as a member of the [[Corps of 40\|Corps of 40]] especially when he teamed up with [[Nasiruddin Mahmud\|Nasiruddin Mahmud]] and his mother to conspire against [[Alauddin Masud\|Alauddin Masud]].

### Under Nasir's Reign
Under the reign of [[Nasiruddin Mahmud\|Nasiruddin Mahmud]] as the 8th Sultān, Balban was appointed as the Naib Sultān and although Balban enjoyed the fruits of power, his greed and ego could not stop him from trying to take over [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] as soon as his chance came.

### Overthrowing Nasir to become Sultān
In 1322 [[Vikram Samvat Calendar\|VS]] (1266 AD), Nasir was overthrown by Ghiyas ud-Din Balban so that he himself could become Sultan. One of the first things Ghiyas did was to start ordering the assassinations of various [[Corps of 40\|Corps of 40]] members.

Many members of the Corps were assassinated and many started to surrender. He realised that the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] is technically a [[Sultanate\|Sultanate]] because no one wanted to change the name of all the titles due to the inconvenience it would cause. Ghiyas used this to his advantage when he asserted his [[Divine Right to Rule\|Divine Right to Rule]] as a representative of Allah and effectively made Allah as the Caliph of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]].

This was not a decision made to plug some hole or gap in the beauracracy but it was to create a system which cannot oppose his narcissism. Due to Balban being a representive of Allah, neither any [[Maulvi\|Maulvi]] nor any [[Maulana\|Maulana]] nor any [[Imam\|Imam]] could ever say that offering [[Sajda\|Sajda]] and [[Paibos\|Paibos]] to Ghiyas was against Islam.

### Death & Aftermath
Despite his narcissistic streak, it is very well known that he loved his son Prince Mohammad a lot (just like a lot of parents since time immemorial). When the prince suddenly died in they year 1342 of [[Vikram Samvat Calendar\|Vikram Samvat Calendar]] (1286 AD), Balban also passed away with shock upon hearing the news.

The offer to rule as the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] was made to his son [[Nasiruddin Bughra Khan\|Nasiruddin Bughra Khan]] but Bughra refused the offer in the favour of his son & Ghiyasuddin's grandson [[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] to rule who accepted the offer to become the next Sultān of Delhi. Qaiqabad's inneficiency to rule had become a laughable menace and he became a puppet of his minister [[Wazir Nijamuddin\|Wazir Nijamuddin]].

[[Nasiruddin Bughra Khan\|Nasiruddin Bughra Khan]] decided to take an army and end the ruckus created by his son. 
Nijamuddin advised [[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] to take an army of his own & confront his father. The forces of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] and the [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] stood opposite to each other, ready to battle against each other but the father & son reached compromise to avoid war. Nijamuddin was fired from being the Wazir/Minister & both went back to their lands.

[[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] was defeated soon after by [[Jalaluddin Khilji\|Jalaluddin Khilji]] of the [[Khilji Dynasty\|Khilji Dynasty]].
This resulted in [[Nasiruddin Bughra Khan\|Bughra Khan]] renouncing power for his other son [[Ruknuddin Kaikus\|Ruknuddin Kaikus]] to run the [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] as an independent Sultanate.
