# Economics
> A subject in Social Science that studies production, distribution and consumption of [[Goods\|Goods]] and Services.

I have studied the subject thrice but was not taking notes on this subject until I studied the subject for the third time. Economics has majorly a few sub-parts which it can be divided into. 
The three major parts are: [[Macro Economics\|Macro Economics]], [[Micro Economics\|Micro Economics]] and [[Statistical Mathematics (Stats)\|Statistics]] while stats is common with Mathematics but in school, I was taught Stats as a part of Economics in 11th grade but with Maths in 9th grade and earlier.

### Types of Economies
[[Socialist Economy\|Socialist Economy]] - Government-Driven Economy ran by govt wellfare companies
[[Capitalist Economy\|Capitalist Economy]] - Market-Driven Economy ran by private companies
[[Mixed Economy\|Mixed Economy]] - Mixture of both.

[[Foreign Trade Policy (2023)\|Foreign Trade Policy (2023)]]
[[Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)\|Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)]] - outward remittance from India to another country. Limits the outward remittance to $250,000 per year unless it is an NRI then the limit gets increased to $1,000,000 per year mainly used by those who want to go outside to study & their family is sending them money - a lot of the times it is seen that they invest in foreign countries with this money. 
[[Dollarisation of an Economy\|Dollarisation of an Economy]] - a situation where a foreign country other than the USA accepts the US Dollar as a legal tender. This removes all control of monetary policy from a country & hands it to the USA.
[[Tariff-based Trade Barriers\|Tariff-based Trade Barriers]] - really heavy [[Import Duty\|Import Duty]] 
[[Non-Tariff Trade Barriers\|Non-Tariff Trade Barriers]] - via quotas (import duty on import only if quota exceeded) and quality control methods.
[[Preferrential Trade Agreement (PTA)\|Preferrential Trade Agreement (PTA)]] where there is a positive list of certain preferential products which is not be tariffed or minimally tariffed. 
[[Free Trade Agreements (FTAs)\|Free Trade Agreements (FTAs)]] limits a negative list of products which will NOT be given preferential access.
[[Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA)\|Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA)]] is a document that takes trade to next level increasing technical cooperation, investments and trade.
[[Custom Union\|Custom Union]] which has many members working together to synchonise their [[Import Duty\|Tariffs]] on external nations.
[[Money Market\|Money Market]] - investment term period is more than 365 days - [[Treasury Bills (T-Bills)\|Zero Coupon Bonds]], [[Certificates of Deposits (CDs)\|Certificates of Deposits (CDs)]] and [[Commercial Paper\|Commercial Paper]] (unsecured promised note). T-bills are issued at a discount on the face value.
[[Capital Markets\|Capital Markets]] - investment term period is greater than 365 days i.e., [[Stock Market\|Stock Market]] & [[Mutual Funds\|MFs]].
[[Initial Public Offering (IPO)\|Initial Public Offering (IPO)]] - Merchant Banker helps decide pricing & 3 days to list & then Bidding period of 3 days & shares are allocated on pro-rata basis.
[[Further Public Offering (FPO)\|Further Public Offering (FPO)]] - further shares being sold after the IPO.
