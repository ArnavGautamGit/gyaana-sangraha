# Don't resign while on PIP
> Often people resign while they are on a [[Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)\|Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)]] which leaves a bad stain on your [[Background Verification (BGV)\|Background Verification (BGV)]] which leaves a stain on you.

[[Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)\|Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)]] also known as Performance Review is something that one is put on when their performance is to be tested. PIP is meant to make you quit since the decision of letting you go has already been made by your manager and the HR - it is only a measure to make you quit yourself.

If you quit on the PIP as they want you to, they can very easily say that you resigned during performance review. A better approach is to stay and get clear deliverables similar to what we learnt in the previous note: [[Handle the Situation when your Boss is trying to Push You Out\|Handle the Situation when your Boss is trying to Push You Out]] - you ask for specific deliverables and what success looks like in your response to make sure you are at least seen as someone who tries to fix their mistakes instead of running from them (even if you believe you are not in the wrong).

Most of the times managers have never thought of what your deliverables, timelines and success is going to be like and once they give either what you demanded or his incomptance in writing on his response email you get a good idea from them on how you can improve. Even if you leave after this, you do not burn your bridges, you preserve them and minimise the damage to your BGV
