# Second Battle of Tarain (1192)
> Second large-scale battle fought between the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rājpūts]] led by [[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] against the [[Ghurid Empire\|Ghurid Empire]] led by [[Mohammad Ghori\|Mohammad Ghori]]. It was the first battle where the rest of the confederacy had not supported the Chauhans.

[[Mohammad Ghori\|Mohammad Ghori]] had lost a war against Prithviraj before in the [[First Battle of Tarain (1191)\|First Battle of Tarain (1191)]] and had realised that fighting melee will not help, so he took the help of deceit. He stayed afar and used bows more than swords. 

This victory cemented his presence in India via his general [[Qutub ud-Din Aibak\|Qutub ud-Din Aibak]] who would establish the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] in Delhi and start the construction of [[Qutub Minar\|Qutub Minar]] in the city of Rai Pithora set up by [[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraj]] (which is now absorbed into the modern city of Delhi).

### Army Size Comparisons
Many historians like Kaushik Roy in books such as [Military Transition in Early Modern Asia](https://books.google.co.in/books?id=KyVnAwAAQBAJ&pg=PA23&redir_esc=y#v=onepage&q&f=false) parrot the claim of "we can speculate that the Rajput army was numerically superior" without ever given the basis of such a speculation and quoting the Islamic sources (from Ghori's side) verbatim. Since he is an Indian/Indian-origin voice people discredit [[Prithvirāja Rāso\|Prithvirāja Rāso]] and associated Rajput works which mention that Ghori had a bigger army.

### Technological Differences
In India, much older texts had advised to use bamboo bows - such as the [[Agni Purāṇa\|Agni Purāṇa]] and the [[Vashishta Dhanurveda\|Vashishta Dhanurveda]] advised on using bamboo among all the sources of bow materials available.

Ghori brought with him Mounted Cavalry from the [[Turkic People\|Turkic]] and [[Turkmen Tribe\|Turkmen]] origin.
Turkmen people now live in Turkmenistan and similar Central Asian tribes like the [[Tatars\|Tatars]] or [[Cumans\|Cumans]] who got famously involved in Europe later during the [[Hungarian Invasion of Bohemia (1402)\|Invasion of Bohemia]] in 1402) but they were present and involved in wars across both Asia & Europe since the 12th Century.

These turkic people used [[Medieval Horn Bow\|smaller bows made using bull horn]]. Since cows, bulls and buffaloes are all considered sacred animals by hindus, these bows were unheard of and actively unused. Due to their smaller size than bamboo bows they worked better on mounted horseback cavalry which worked for the Ghurid Cavalry.

### Political Division among the Rajputs
It is said that since Prithviraj loved the daughter of another rajput king ruling the regions in present-day UP and Bihar, [[Jayachandra (Jaichand)\|Jayachandra (Jaichand)]] who opposed the marriage. 

The two lovers decided to elope and marry in Ajmer, the capital of Prithviraj's kingdom ruled by the dynasty of [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chauhan Rajputs of Ajmer]]. Prithviraj was 22 at the time but they could not elope yet because the Ghurid threat was rising that year in 1191 so when the Chauhan King won the [[First Battle of Tarain (1191)\|First Battle of Tarain (1191)]], he decided to elope with his lover.

He did not forsee the [[Second Battle of Tarain (1192)\|Second Battle of Tarain (1192)]] and had nothing to do with [[Mohammad Ghori\|Ghori]] and his [[Ghurid Empire\|Ghurid Empire]] but this move had angered the girl's father Jaichand who opposed the marriage earlier. It was Jaichand who convinced the other Rajputs to not support Prithviraj when Prithviraj tried to ask other kings in the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]] for military aid against a large army.

Since this time he was left isolated, he passed away while [[Jayachandra (Jaichand)\|Jaichand]]'s kingdom was next after the [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chauhans of Ajmer]] had fallen, this time it was too late since the largest of the Rajput kingdoms under the Chauhans had fallen. They could have done nothing now. 

### Aftermath of the War
Many Rajput dynasties such as the [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandela Rajputs]] never recovered from losing this wars and ended. Monuments such as [[Qila Rai Pithora\|Qila Rai Pithora]] and the [[Khajuraho Group of Temples\|Khajuraho Group of Temples]] were either destroyed or fell to ruin.
