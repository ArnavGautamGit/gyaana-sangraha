# Lodhi Dynasty
> The final dynasty to rule the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] ever in its history.

The only dynasty to come to power without war in the Sultanate's history and one of the few around the world to have achieved this feat. They came to power when [[Alauddin Alam Shah\|Alauddin Alam Shah]] of the preceeding [[Sayyid Dynasty\|Sayyid Dynasty]] surrendered his throne to [[Bahlol Lodhi\|Bahlol Lodhi]] without war in 1451.

Their comparitively more tolerant policies for Hindus and [[Sikandar Lodhi\|Sikandar Lodhi]]'s founding of the city of Agra in they year 1504 AD is one of the reasons cited that they have a garden named after them called "Lodhi Garden" in Delhi - although there is talk to change its name since at the end of the day these guys were invaders anyway.

Bahlol Lodhi proceeds to expand the sultanate upto the edge of Jaunpur in present-day UP while his successor Sikandar expands the sultanate to include parts of present-day Bihar.

### Sequence of Sultāns
23. [[Bahlol Lodhi\|Bahlol Lodhi]] ---> 1451 AD to 1489 AD
24. [[Sikandar Lodhi\|Sikandar Lodhi]] ---> 1489 AD to 1517 AD
25. [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] ---> 1517 AD to 1526 AD

### Decline
Ultimately the Lodhi Dynasty was brought to an end in [[First Battle of Panipat (1526)\|First Battle of Panipat (1526)]] when [[Babur\|Babur]] invaded India with [[Early Firearms\|Early Firearms]] and [[Cannon Artillery\|Cannon Artillery]] to establish the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Empire]]. This war also ended the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] permanently but the term Sultanat/Saltanat was often used for the Mughals too in contemporary & vernacular texts of other neighbouring kingdoms.
