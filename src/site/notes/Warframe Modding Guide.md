# Warframe Modding Guide
> Modding is an activity in [[Warframe\|Warframe]] to add specific mods that boost certain atributes about your warframe and your weapons or any other thing that has mods such as companions.

[[Warframe\|Warframe]] as a game is very Modding Dependent and it dictates how much damage you can deal in the game since base damage for weapons is shit apart from the one exception being [[Nataruk\|Nataruk]] which you unlock lategame and even that will need to be modded eventually.

### Key Things to know
Warframes have two layers of survival - one is shield and the second is health. Shields are blue and Health is red, while Shields can by regenerated and shields hitting zero is no problem. Most warframes also have armour but it is so small and cannot be generated or picked up it is useless to mod for.

Sometimes, certain frames like [[Inaros\|Inaros]] have no shields, only health.
Othertimes many like [[Dante\|Dante]] have [[Overguard\|Overguard]] (it protects you from knockdowns as well) which needs to be taken out before one can reach the shields and health. All this info is given on the top right when starting a mission.

Each Warframe has 4 abilities, depending upon the specific warframe they could also be tactical or damage based or the could be completely support based which is best used in tandem with other warframes. Example, [[Excalibur\|Excalibur]] has a great set of abilities for high damage such as the Exalted Blade while [[Volt\|Volt]] has a lot of support abilities like Electric Shield. Abilities require a resource known as energy which is showcased on the bottom right when starting a mission

Mini-map is on the top left and bottom left has the name of the weapon you are using alongside its ammo. The format in which ammo is listed is the same as other Shooter Games. Apart from the map itself anything else you can see on the UI when on a mission, everything can be picked up by killing enemies.


### Modding the Warframes
When Modding the Warframes in the game, it is important to note that we are modding for the following things:
1. Survivability - Vitality and Redirection are two mods that will boost health (Red) and shields (Blue) but there are many others like Augur Accord.
2. Ability Spamming - Energy regeneration so Abilities can be spammed, ability strength and efficiency.
	1. If an ability that drains energy is active then energy regeneration mods are useless
	2. If the warframe has ability that is timed such as [[Oberon\|Oberon]]'s Hallowed Grounds then ability duration helps but do't go too hard on it
	3. Ability Casters (whose abilities do not drain energy constantly as they are active) do not benefit from energy regeneration much since you will get more from pickups after killing enemies than anything else.
3. Damage Type - some specific mods like Chromatic Blade which is exclusive to [[Excalibur\|Excalibur]] that can alter the damage type based on the energy colour you selected for the Exalted Blade when customising.
4. Mission Specific Extras - sometimes you may want to have mods like Enemy Radar or Loot Detector to highlight enemies or pickups on your minimap since nothing is highlighted on the map unless modded for. 
	1. Since you do have directional damage like other shooters games where direction of the shot is specified on the UI, you would be able to see where you are being hit from anyway, so keep these on lowest priority.

### Mod Capacity and Forma!
As you level up your Warframe More, you will find that the maximum mod capacity increases until you hit max rank at level 30 for your warframe. You can double your mod capacity of your warframe with the Orokin Reactor bought from Nightwave. For doubling Weapon Mod capacity you would need the Orokin Catalyst. They are often called yellow and blue potatoes respectively.

Forma is also an interesting item. Its blueprint is farmable via [[Void Relics\|Void Relics]].
Forma is a resource that will add a polarity on your mod slot meaning mods matching the polarity will take up half the mod capacity. But, applying a forma will reset the weapon's level back to unranked (level zero).

### Modding Weapons
Not just your Warframes themselves, even your weapons and your vehicles such as [[Railjack\|Railjack]] can be modded. Railjacks are quite late game in the story but Weapon modding is covered in the next note of the beginner's guide: [[Elemental Damage\|Elemental Damage]].
