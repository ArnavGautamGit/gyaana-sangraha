# Chalukya-Pallava Wars
> Series of wars fought between the [[Chalukya Dynasty\|Chalukya Dynasty]] and [[Pallava Dynasty\|Pallava Dynasty]] in Central and Deccan India in the 7th Century AD and 8th Century AD 

Chalukyas ruled the region of present day Karnataka and parts of Maharashtra and Kerala (now renamed to Keralam in 2026) at the peak of their power. The modern city of Badami was their capital city back then known as "Vatapi". The founder of this dynasty is largely believed to be [[Pulakeshin I\|Pulakeshin I]] while the most powerful leader was [[Pulakeshin II\|Pulakeshin II]] who ruled from 606 to 642 AD and won against Pallavas many times.

Pallavas were centered around Telangana on the opposite coast of the Indian Peninsula founded by [[Mahendravarman I\|Mahendravarman I]] and controlled parts of Odisha, Chattisgarh, Andhra Pradesh at their peak under [[Narsimhavarman I\|Narsimhavarman I]], the son of the Pallava founder. Mahendravarman got slain in battle in 630 AD with [[Pulakeshin II\|Pulakeshin II]] which fueled Narsimhavarman's revenge. He ended up quenching his thirst for revenge almost 12 years later in 642 AD by killing [[Pulakeshin II\|Pulakeshin II]]. 

Narsimhavarman even captures the city of Vatapi to get the title of "Conqueror of Vatapi" or also called "Vatapikonda". This puts a stop on wars until the turn of the century.

### Cultural Contributions & Administration of both sides
Chalukyas built the Mahabalipuram and Mahabaleshwar Temples while the Pallavas built Kanchipuram and many surrounding temple-oriented cities.

Both of them had a centralised administration style but the Pallavas had a separate village named Sabha which was reserved for the Brahmins while the other villages categorised as ur can be resided by anyone.

### Resumption of hostilities & the aftermath
When the Chalukyan ruler [[Vikramāditya III\|Vikramāditya III]] came to power, he made sure to completely wipe the Pallavas clean off the map by 740 AD in successive wars and victories - all of which were led by his military commander [[Dāntidurga\|Dāntidurga]].

The Chalukyas split into two branches of royal families, the [[Eastern Chalukya Dynasty\|Eastern Chalukya Dynasty]] ruled from the city of Vengi (instead of the Pallava capital of Kanchipuram). while the Western branch still kept ruled from Badami/Vatapi.

Chalukyas retained power until King [[Vikramāditya III\|Vikramāditya III]] started to grow old leading to their commander [[Dāntidurga\|Dāntidurga]] amassing more power with political alliances in Vikramāditya's court trying to strengthen his own candidate's claim over the others (who Dāntidurga eventually even married off his daughter to).

Unfortunately things did not go his way and [[Kirtivarman II\|Kirtivarman II]] became the new king leading to [[Dāntidurga\|Dāntidurga]] leading a coup against him, ceasing the existance of the Western Chalukya branch and starting his new [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]] and we here we setup the stage for the future big conflict known as the [[Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj\|Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj]].
