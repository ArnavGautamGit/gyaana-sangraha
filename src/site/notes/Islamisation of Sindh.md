# Islamisation of Sindh
> Process of rapid conversion, atrocities of Hindus, Sikhs and non-muslims of Sindh after the Hindu Brahmin [[Chach Dynasty of Sindh\|Chach Dynasty of Sindh]] lost to the massive [[Ummayyad Caliphate\|Ummayyad Caliphate]] in war as described in the Persian Text [[Chāch Nāmā\|Chāch Nāmā]].

This process of Islamic hegemony and non-muslim opppression began from the moment the muslims won. Some people of Aror city in Sindh left northwards into Punjab to save themselves and hence the Arora surname became common among the Sindhis and Punjabis. 

Similarly, other people from other cities fled to different locations eastwards into present-day India into Kathiwar & Saurashtra controlled by the [[Maitrakas of Saurashtra & Vallabhi\|Maitrakas of Saurashtra & Vallabhi]] as well. This is why many Sindhi surnames are also found in Gujarat, those that were left were slowly bankrupted into accepting Islam or killed. the lucky few survived by continuing to pay [[Jizya\|Jizya]].

Over time, Islam became the majority religion in Sindh.
