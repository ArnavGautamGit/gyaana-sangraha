# Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj
> Also called as "Kannauj Wars" and "Tripartite Struggle" was a series of wars fought between the three empires: [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]], [[Gurjara-Pratihara Empire\|Gurjara-Pratihara Empire]] and [[Pala Dynasty of Bengal\|Pala Dynasty of Bengal]].

These were fought along a period from around 785 AD to 815 AD for about 30 years. It was a heavy struggle over the city and surrounding region for Kannauj over the power vacuum left behind after the collapse of [[Pushyabhuti Dynasty\|Pushyabhuti Dynasty]] and [[Mukhari Dynasty of Gaya & Kannauj\|Mukhari Dynasty of Gaya & Kannauj]].

Regions of present Gujarat and Rajasthan were controlled by the Gurjara-Pratihara while Rashtrakutas controlled much of Deccan. Palas controlled Bengal and Bihar. At the same time the regions of Malwa, in present-day MP and all of present-day UP was an absolute warzone.

[[Gurjara-Pratihara Empire\|Gurjara-Pratihara Empire]] was made up of multiple royal/noble families who later took on the identity of "Rajputs" including the [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandela Rajputs]], [[Parmar Rajputs\|Parmar Rajputs]] among other families... one of the weirdest and most fascinating pieces of Military History in India.

This conflict made sure all three emerging empires exhaust themselves in war, it all ending with cross-marriages after some peace was brokered (but not for long) as when these three empires collapsed, all those families that identified with the Rajput identity formed the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]]. Everyone forgot about [[Islam\|Islam]] knocking on the door from the side of Sindh & Afghanistan.
