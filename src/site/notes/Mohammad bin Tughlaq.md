# Mohammad bin Tughlaq
> Second Ruler from the [[Tughlaq Dynasty\|Tughlaq Dynasty]] and 16th [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] who came to power in 1326 AD after "accidentally" assassinating his father [[Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq\|Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq]]. 

Born as "Jauna Khan" but also known as "The Mad Sultān" he was known for his 5 major experiments that all failed spectacularly. 

### Summary of his reign
He realised that barbarians like his lot are best suited at war so he came in and started to raid and destroy southern kingdoms and expanded the kingdom to its zenith by around 1330 AD (which is 3.5 years into his reign) and unlike [[Alauddin Khilji\|Alauddin Khilji]] of the [[Khilji Dynasty\|Khilji Dynasty]] before him, he established permanent control by displacing local dynasties permanently.

Later he would get comfortable in his reign, enjoying all sorts of luxuries & obviously since these barbarians are not used to sitting in peace (and meditation is haraam) he started to try weird experiments which he tries to force upon the people using his military might. This still has connotations in the modern day where a "Tughlaqi Farmān" refers to a senseless authoritarian dictat which overlooks the common people's point of view for abstract strategic gains that are often not proven to be backed by any evidence apart from hearsay.

These Experiments led to [[Vijayanagara Empire\|Vijayanagara Empire]] and recently established [[Bahmani Sultanate\|Bahmani Sultanate]] to assert themselves as new independent kingdoms in 1336 AD & 1347 AD respectively that are independent from the Tughlaqs ruling the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]].

Although I would rather use the term "Five Blunders" but I would stay polite because I was raised well & not like a barbarian. I will stick to the term "Five Experiments". 

### Experiment 1 - Capital change to Devgiri (1327 AD)
The first failed experiment was moving the capital from Delhi towards Central India in 1327 so he could better administer the central & southern regions (since he wanted to establish complete control over these regions now). 

He tried moving the capital to a hill-town named Devgiri (name translates to "Mountain of Gods") since hills create a natural moat around a castle. He prompty renamed the city to Daulatabad before he made it the capital. The new name translated to "Town of Money/Wealth" meaning that the Tughlaqs no only wanted to remove the Hindu symbols but also the fact that money is more important to them than the divine. 

People of all walks of life were forced to move to Devgiri on a 1500 km journey which caused many casualties & death due to the summer heat (which only increased as they moved south towards the Tropic of Cancer i.e., inwards into the Tropical Zone from the Temperate climate of Delhi).

Tughlaq's reversal on this decision meant that surviving people were unable to travel back just due to the fear that they might die. This left Delhi as a "ghost town" with a small nuber of people who couldn't afford to travel to Devgiri the first time around anyway.

### Experiment 2 - Token Currency (1329 AD)
The second failed experiment was to introduce Bronze and Copper as the token currency in 1329. This meant that their new bronze/copper coins were priced the same as their old Silver Tankas. This collapsed due to the coins lacking any security features meaning that there were no identifiers that could separate officially minted coins & would be impossible to reproduce without the modern implements. The widespread forgery of coins that followed forced the leadership to revert their decision.

### Experiment 3 - Higher Taxing during a famine!
The third blunderous experiment being that the tax on produce in the Ganga-Yamuna Doab region was increased during a famine! Were the people suppose to give more of the already scarce produce to the king or feed their children?

### Experiment 4 - Khorasan Expedition
Next up being the blunder of raising an army of 370,000 soldiers to invade Central Asia in partnership with the [[Shahs of Khwarazm\|Shahs of Khwarazm]] to attack Khorasan specifically but control as much Central Asia as possible. Eventually the Khwarazmi Shahs came to a good diplomatic standing with Khorasan & most central asian powers.

Feeding a standing army that firstly has no purpose & secondly cannot be fired (due to a spike in unemployment) would mean that Tughlaq was stuck. To correct the blunder caused by this experiment, he decided to undergo another one...

### Experiment 5 - Qarachil Expedition
Tughlaq decided that since he needs to send this army to fight somewhere, let's invade Uttarakhand & Himachal Pradesh... erstwhile civilians of the mountainous regions banded together with their army to defend the honour of their mountain shrines from these barbarians.

The War went badly for the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] and Uttarakhand won handsomely as only a few of those 370,000 returned home after the war (as all of the others were killed by the army of soldiers, civilians, tribals, temple priests and ascetics)

It is not clear whether the Qarachil tribe or people or location they targeted was in modern day Kumaon or Garhwal. Some link it to be around Kullu & Kangra regions of the Himachal Pradesh while others link it to being in the Kumaon regions of Uttarakhand.

It can be understood that both would have been targeted since many historians believed it was either a first step to secure a frontier for attacking China or to at least stop/blockade the trade of horses into China from Central Asia. All of these historians were muslims like [[Mohammed Qasim Hindu Shah (Firishta)\|Firishta]] and [[Ziauddin Barani\|Ziauddin Barani]], so it could well be [[Al-Taqiya\|Al-Taqiya]].

### Death & Secession
Due to his poor policies and management, he was such a bad ruler that more & more regions were revolting against him by the day & declaring themselves Independent Kingdoms.

Jauna Khan/Mohammad bin Tughlaq died in 1351 AD while battling the Sindh rebels who wanted an independent kingdom again. It is unclear whether he died on the battlefield, was assassinated in his camp or passed away due to not being able to take the discomfort of being in a tent in the wild...

Since he died without any heirs and had no siblings, it was a frenzy on who inherits the throne & eventually [[Feroz Shah Tughlaq\|Feroz Shah Tughlaq]] made it to being the successor of Jauna Khan.
