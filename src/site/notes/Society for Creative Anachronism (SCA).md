# Society for Creative Anachronism (SCA)
> It is a form of Historical Reenactment inspired from life before the 17th Century. However this does not mean that people are supposed to represent historic empires or figures one to one. The SCA has its own universe and lore to avoid political and cultural conflicts apart from being as inclusive as possible.

To avoid political conflicts and cultural conflicts, they have made their own universe with its distinct lore where they welcome all cultures to take part. This is because mirroring historical empires 1:1 might also recreate historical conflicts in today's world. The SCA's universe is referred to as The Known World of the SCA (sometimes Known is spelled as "Knowne")

To see how kingdoms are divided, we have a section in this page named "Kingdoms & History of the SCA"

### Membership to the SCA
Membership fee must be paid to the society to be a part of the same but one may not need to become a member to attend any nearby event. There is a ticket price that is charged on a per-person basis. Members do get a discount on event entry free of around $10 USD and a bunch of other benefits that are best seen on [the official SCA Wesbite](https://www.sca.org)

It can be understood as the membership to the SCA being akin to a subscription one gets for other services like streaming services for movies/music.

### SCA Groups by Size
Initially any group begins as a "shire" i.e., small village. To register a shire one needs to have at least 5 paying members of the SCA. The 5 positions are clearly defined:
1. Seneschal (President/Organiser)
2. Herald
3. Minister of Arts & Sciences
4. Exchequer (Treasurer)
5. Minister of Combat Safety (also sometimes referred to "Marshal")

When there are 25 paying members who have subscribed to the SCA, you get upgraded to a Principality and then when you have more than 150 you can apply to become a baronny. A kingdom is the largest set which must have a size of 400+ paying members.

When a group expands, new roles also come up to the picture:
1. Chronicler (documents the group's history)
2. Youth Officer (for youth activities)
3. Webminister (manages the Group's Website and other internet tech)
4. Chartelaine (connects newcomers to point people to get started)
5. Social Media Officer (promotes events and moderates discourse on discord and similar services)

Note that there is also a canton which is similar to a shire but it is NOT independent and reports to a baronny. Note that becoming the King/Queen/Baron/Baroness is separate from being the Seneschal of the Kingdom/Baronny/Principality/Shire. The Seneschal is the organiser.

The Board of Directors (BOD) of the SCA as a whole supercede the powers of any kingdom. So they stand above the Crown in terms of powers but most of the time the only power that they do exercise is to approve the addition of new kingdoms.

### Kingdoms & History of the SCA
Kindom of the West or Kingdom of West was the first kingdom near California in the backyard of a few friends who just wanted to have fun in 1966, then there were multiple events that happened in the first 2 kingdoms itself since the idea of historical recreation was a hit among folks.

Then they had eventually come up with newer political terms to define the ranks of the groups by the number of paying members that are a part of the group.

Eventually the East Coast became its own kingdom named Kingdom of the East.
Kingdom of Atlantia was once a part of Kindom of the East but broke away from it.

Slowly different kingdoms began spawning up on the Eastern Coast starting with the Kingdom of the East. Atlantia broke away from the East & formed its own Kingdom. Similarly other kingdoms slowly broke away from the original 2 Kingdoms.
