# Bailment
> Concept in Legal Studies & Law where an item that is the subject of bailment still belongs to the original owner despite being handled by someone else on their behalf and the original owner can reclaim and snatch the item from the handler at any time.

In simpler words, it is similar to how when a person goes to a Valet Parking and hands over their keys, they have a reasonable expectation of getting their key back instead of the valet guy now suddenly getting the rights to own the car i.e., the original owner retains the right to own the vehicle and reclaim the vehicle as it was (without damage or changes) while the Valet Service is just the handler of the vehicle.

It is used in many cases of litigation - especially the [[Surveillance Accountability Act (SAA, 2026)\|Surveillance Accountability Act (2026)]] in the USA. I have not seen this concept in other countries simply because I have not read enough cases and I am not a lawyer.
