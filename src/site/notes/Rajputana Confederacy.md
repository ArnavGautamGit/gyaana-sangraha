# Rājpūtānā Confederacy
> A confederacy of kingly states ruled by kings from the Rajput community. This was preceded by a much top-down almost vassalising status of smaller kingdoms seen in the precursor known as the [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]].

Originally, the Rajput community had origins in various different castes but since according to Hinduism, caste is based on work & occupation rather than birth (this opinion is concurred by the [[Shrimad-Bhagavad Gītā\|Shrimad-Bhagavad Gītā]] 4:13 and 18:41-44). Due to their martial nature, back then and even today Rajputs were considered among the Kshtriyas. Jatts/Jaats are also considered Kshatriya for this reason.

### Structure
The Rajputana Confederates were all those who were ruled by a ruler from the Rajput Community except some outliers (if any). The confederates had autonomy and would often lend each other support. The strongest confederate (member state) with the largest army and most supporters would often run the entire confederacy since they could sway the room since important decisions often called for a meeting.

The confederacy was run by multiple Rajput Clans where each clan ruled a kingdom, some clans were powerful enough to rule multiple surrounding kingdoms. The most significant and largest clans include the names of:
- [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhans]] 
- [[Guhila Rajputs\|Guhilas]] 
- [[Parmar Rajputs\|Parmars]] 
- [[Pratihara Rajputs\|Pratiharas]] 
- [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandelas]]
- [[Tomara Rajputs\|Tomaras]] 

There may be other royal clans of different royal families that I might have missed and I am extremely sorry if that is the case. For the sake of brevity, I will include the Sisodia Rajputs as a cadet branch of the Guhilas especially in case of the [[Kingdom of Mewar\|Kingdom of Mewar]].

Since there were many families and clans that created a confederacy they were famously divided and not united enough to help each other in the time of need. For Example, the rest of the confederacy did not come up to help the [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratiharas]] and the [[Solanki Dynasty\|Solanki Dynasty]] during the [[Ghaznavid Invasion of India\|Ghaznavid Invasion of India]]. Great powers such as the [[Parmar Rajputs\|Parmar Rajputs]] under [[King Bhoja\|King Bhoja]] did not help either.

### Power & Extent
The confederacy was at its zenith from the ending of the [[Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj\|Kannauj Tripartite Wars]] in late 800s to the [[First Battle of Tarain (1191)\|First Battle of Tarain (1191)]]. The confederates were either wiped or vassalised in 1191 by the [[Ghurid Empire\|Ghurid Empire]] which later became the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]]. The politics and powerplay involved in these wars will be covered in their respective notes. 

Although it is to be noted that different confederate states may have different peaks in power, for example the states under the [[Pratihara Rajputs\|Pratihara Rajputs]] were on a decline in the 1100s while other states were near their peak.

### Cultural Contributions
Apart from major temples such as [[Khajuraho Group of Temples\|Khajuraho Group of Temples]] and [[Somnath Jyotirlinga\|Somnath Jyotirlinga]], the Rajputs mainstreamed the idea of bravery, chivalry and patriotism. They were great patrons of Sanskrit Literature & scholarship by patronising multiple works like historical accounts such as [[Rājatarangini\|Rājatarangini]] written by Kalhan (chronicling the history of Kashmir), literary landmarks which influenced the [[Bhakti Movement\|Bhakti Movement]] such as Jayadeva's [[Gītā Govindam\|Gītā Govindam]] and even royal biographies like [[Prithvirāja Rāso\|Prithvirāja Rāso]] on the life of [[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] who ruled from Ajmer as the king of the [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chahamanas of Shakambari]] branch of the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]]. Throughout all these time periods, they were also great patrons of [[Indian Miniature Paintings\|Indian Miniature Paintings]] especially the subset known as [[Rajputana Miniature Paintings\|Rajputana Miniature Paintings]].

### Decline and Aftermath
After losing the [[Second Battle of Tarain (1192)\|Second Battle of Tarain (1192)]] due to internal politics, many confederates were either killed or vassalised by the [[Ghurid Empire\|Ghurid Empire]] and the resulting [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]]. Most of the confederacy took the side of the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Empire]] later except the kingdom of Mewar ruled by Maharana [[Pratap Singh I\|Pratap Singh I]] who stood up against Akbar.

Later, certain Rajputana Kingdoms supported the [[Maratha Empire\|Maratha Empire]] as well although many chose not to do so due to revenue reasons i.e., paying the [[Jizya\|Jizya]] (1/25th of Revenue) was easier than paying the [[Chauth\|Chauth]] (1/4th of revenue) demanded by the [[Maratha Empire\|Maratha Empire]].
