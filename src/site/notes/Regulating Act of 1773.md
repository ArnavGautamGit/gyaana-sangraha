# Regulating Act of 1773
> An Act passed in the British Parliament in the year 1773 to regulate the [[British East India Company\|British East India Company]] and divide its jurisdiction properly.

Under this legislation, the position of "Governor of Bengal" would be renamed to the designation of "Governor-General of Bengal" and act as the sole point of communication between India and the EIC's higher ups sitting in Britain and the Governor General Executive Council (GGEC) of 4 members was set up to assist the Governor-General of Bengal (hereafter referred to as GGB). While [[Robert Clive\|Robert Clive]]  was the first Governor of Bengal, [[Warren Hastings\|Warren Hastings]] looted as the first [[Governor-General of Bengal\|Governor-General of Bengal]].

Also a new [[Governor-General's Executive Council (GGEC)\|Governor-General's Executive Council (GGEC)]] was setup in Bengal with 4 members in addition to the [[Governor-General of Bengal\|Governor-General of Bengal]] himself.

### Ammeding Act, 1781
8 years later it was ammended to exempt the [[Governor-General of Bengal\|Governor-General of Bengal]] from the jurisdiction of the [[Supreme Court of the United Kingdom\|Supreme Court of the United Kingdom]].
