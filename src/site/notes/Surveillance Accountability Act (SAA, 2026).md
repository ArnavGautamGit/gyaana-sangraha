# Surveillance Accountability Act (SAA, 2026)
> an Act proposed in the USA by privacy activist Naomi Brockwell and the Ludlow Institute where the data that people hand over to Third Parties will no longer be treated under the age-old [[Third Party Doctrine of Data Ownership\|Third Party Doctrine]] but rather as [[Bailment\|Bailment]].

This would mean that all your data that you give to other companies with the reasonable expectation of [[Personal Online Privacy\|Personal Privacy]] will still belong to you (unlike the [[Third Party Doctrine of Data Ownership\|Third Party Doctrine]]) but it will only be handled by the third party in accordance to the agreement signed as a part of the third party's privacy policy.

It is currently going to be introduced to the Lower and Upper houses of the USA's Parliament (Capitol) for it to be debated on after it passes the committee. Most laws die in the committee and they say they have a way to get votes on it to force it out of the committee if the government in power currently tries to sideline the law.
