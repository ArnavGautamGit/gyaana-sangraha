# Nasiruddin Bughra Khan
> The son of [[Ghiyas ud-Din Balban\|Ghiyas ud-Din Balban]] and the 1st [[Bengal Sultanate\|Sultān of Bengal]].

![[Nasiruddin Bughra Khan.png\|Nasiruddin Bughra Khan.png]]

First person to successfully seutp Bengal as an independent region from [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] that it had its own dynasty of Sultāns. Established the [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] after the death of his father [[Ghiyas ud-Din Balban\|Ghiyas ud-Din Balban]].

### Refusal to leave Bengal to rule Delhi
The offer to rule as the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] was made to him but Bughra refused the offer in the favour of his son & Ghiyasuddin's grandson [[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] to rule. It is possible he saw or heard the kind of frantic chase people had taken up in their attempts to take the throne of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] leading to numerous assassination attempts. Maybe Bughra did not want to live with that fear 24/7. Establishing his son as the Sultān would mean that his Sultanate will be free from the constant political games and other such bullshit.

Qaiqabad accepted the offer to become the next Sultān of Delhi. His inneficiency to rule had become a laughing stock and he became a puppet of his minister [[Wazir Nijamuddin\|Wazir Nijamuddin]].

Nasiruddin Bughra Khan decided to take an army and end the ruckus created by his son. 
Nijamuddin advised [[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] to take an army of his own & confront his father. The forces of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] and the [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] stood opposite to each other, ready to battle against each other but the father & son reached compromise to avoid war. Nijamuddin was fired from being the Wazir/Minister & both went back to their lands.

### Renouncing Power
[[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] was defeated and possibly killed within 3 years of this clash with by a man named [[Jalaluddin Khilji\|Jalaluddin Khilji]] of the [[Khilji Dynasty\|Khilji Dynasty]].

This resulted in Bughra Khan growing tired of political power games and renounced power for his other son [[Ruknuddin Kaikus\|Ruknuddin Kaikus]] to run the [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] as an independent Sultanate.
