# Dhurandhar Part 2 - The Revenge (2026)
![Dhurandhar-_The_Revenge_poster.jpg](/img/user/Vaulted%20Images/Dhurandhar-_The_Revenge_poster.jpg)
> Although other posters exist, I have chosen the one that is most similar to the Part 1's poster.

The sequel movie to the Indian Movie [[Dhurandhar Part 1 (2025)\|Dhurandhar]] releasing less than 4 months after the first one (the first movie is on Netflix btw). This was supposed to be a singular movie but was split into two halves sometime during production but despite a change of music partners (from Saregama to T-Series), the music does not disappoint at all.

I have got most of the songs of the movie downloaded as lossless music on my devices (both my laptop and my phone) so that I can easily listen to the songs whenever I want.

### Honest Review
I will not really list down any spoilers here but I will say you are in for a great time & there have been many many many exposés and cameos by famous Indian and Pakistani personalities. There are big reveals and twists that completely unexpected and reassuring. Anyone calling this propaganda should call every work of fiction which is inspired from real events and fills in the gaps with artistic liberty as propaganda.

The film has revolutionised action, the action is just like a Hollywood R-rated movie but with a lot more cursing and gore. Just like the trailer promised, the movie unfurls the past of Hamza on how he went from Jaskirat Singh Rangi to Hamza Ali Mazari while providing a good closure on the movie (how the main story set in pakistan unfurls and finishes).

Cinematography & Peak Storytelling is the same as [[Dhurandhar Part 1 (2025)\|Dhurandhar Part 1 (2025)]] and the immersion masterclass continues throughout this film as well. In the end the only slightly disappointing thing for me was excluding [[Pulwama Terror Attack (2019)\|Pulwama Terror Attack (2019)]] & [[Balakot Airstrike (2019)\|Balakot Airstrike (2019)]] that followed despite mentioning the [[Uri Terror Attack (2016)\|Uri Terror Attack (2016)]] and the [[The Surgical Strike (2016)\|The Surgical Strike (2016)]]. It appears as though that part of the movie underwent reshoots or was intentionally cut to reduce the runtime (could also be the [[Indian Board of Fillm Certification (IBFC)\|IBFC]] issuing cuts). The movie's timeline seems to end around February or March 2025 so it is natural to skip [[Operation Sindoor (2025)\|Operation Sindoor (2025)]] since it happened in May later that year (2 to 3 months later).

### Flawless Attention to Detail & Peak Detailing
Atiq Ahmed (Atif Ahmed in the movie) was an actual muslim MP in India who admitted in his statement to the police that he has ISI links and he gets a hoarde of guns from the [[Inter-Services Intelligence (ISI)\|ISI]] even after his arrest via drones over the India-Pakistan Border or other handlers via the Indo-Nepal border. His assassination is also recreated faithfully in the movie.

The Khanani brothers meet almost the exactly same end in real life as the film shows with Altaf Khanani being arrested by the USA agencies on money laundering charges while the Pakistan-loyalist Javed Khanani jumped or fell from the top of his office building onto a bunch of live wires in real life to die a very similar death as shown in the movie. 
SP Chaudhary Aslam also died the same way in real life as showcased in the movie.

Similarly for characters that are a fictional amalgamation of multiple real life characters, we note that Jaskirat's fighting style of throwing their opponent overboard and using a heavy object to slam into a fallen enemy remains the same even after moving to Pakistan showcasing that lifelong habits cannot be changed with just 2 years of training.

Among cameos Yami Gautam, the wife of the writer-director Aditya Dhar makes a cameo as an Indian Agent while wearing a nameplate that says "Shazia Bano" which is a reference to her previous movie "Haq" made under the same production house. 

### Allegations of being Propaganda of the BJP
Many leaders and spokesperson of political parties which comprise the political opposition in the [[Lok Sabha\|Lower House of the Parliament]] namely the [[Indian National Congress\|Indian National Congress]] and their alliance partners like the [[Samajwadi Party (SP)\|Samajwadi Party (SP)]] have called both Dhurandhar 1 & 2 as propaganda for the ruling dispensation of which the [[Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)\|Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)]] is the largest party.

The allegations are stemmed from the fact that [[Demonetisation (2016)\|Demonetisation (2016)]] was showcased in a positive light and that Prime Minsiter Modi's iconic clip of announcing the Demonetisation was also used in the film. Many also point to how Aditya Dhar has received 2 National Awards from the government since the BJP came to power in 2014.

Many other actors like Shah Rukh Khan have won the national awards in the time Congress was in power and blatantly changed the name of Hindu people like Ranjan Negi to muslim characters in movies like "ChakDe India" to build a divisive propaganda - yet that is dismissed because he favours the left-leaning parties. At the same time, demonetisation's effects was only and solely showcased from a National Security point of view and it is true that rampant counterfeiting of Indian Currency Notes by Pakistan stopped for some time with prominent counterfeiters like Javed Khanani even choosing to commit suicide.

Many point to how connecting real events with fictional characters taking up fictional narratives is something that compromises genuine espionage-work done by true spies, it is important to note that prominent juggernauts of literature such as [[William Shakespeare\|William Shakespeare]] did the same with his works and received multiple accolades by the government of the day despite clearly not being bipartisan in his approach to political themes. What makes Aditya Dhar different to other writers and playwrights of history who (like Shakespeare) have been doing this for ages?
