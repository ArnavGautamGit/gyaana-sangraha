# Chauhan Rajputs (चौहान/चौहाण)
> Also known by their traditional name "Chahamana", the Chauhans are one of the many Rajput Clans that ruled feudal lands in [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]] days as [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chahamanas of Shakambari]] and later became known as "Chauhans of Sambhar" and "Chauhans of Ajmer"

Chahamanas/Chauhans had other royal lineages as well that might be linked on this note as I study more about them. The original sanskrit name "Chāhamāna" is spelled as "चाहमाण" in Hindi and Sanskrit.

### Chavan & other name variations
It is to be noted that the chahamana surname which was known as "Chauhan" in Rajputana regions, was also called as चवन or चवान (spelled most often as Chavan or Chawan although other English spellings exist). This alternate was more popular in central & western India where Marathi and other phonetically similar languages were spoken (including gujarat, where both variants exist since technically it was under the Rajputana)

### Mainline Clan at Ajmer
[[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] had a child named [[Govindaraja IV Chauhan\|Govindaraja IV Chauhan]] who was a minor during the war (probably under the age of 12 or 11) who had agreed for the [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Ajmer Chauhans]] to be the vassal of the Delhi Sultanate until Prithviraja's brother [[Hari Rāma Chauhan\|Hari Rāma Chauhan]] came to dethrone him and lead a revolution against the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]]. It can be assumed that Hari told his nephew to flee to Ranastambhapura (now called "Ranthambore") and showcase to the Sultanate how he is loyal to the throne so that his life can be spared. This assumption is attested by the fact that when the revolution failed Hari was easily granted asylum by his nephew ruling Ranthambore.

[[Govindaraja IV Chauhan\|Govindaraja IV Chauhan]] founded the [[Chahamanas of Ranastambhapura\|Chahamanas of Ranastambhapura]] branch of the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]] which went on till the [[Siege of Ranstambhapura (1301)\|Siege of Ranstambhapura (1301)]] led by the Sultan of Delhi [[Alauddin Khilji\|Alauddin Khilji]]

### Mainline Clan's other offshoots at Jalor & Naddula
Some time in 11th century, a new branch at [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Jalor]] (1160-1311) came to be from the smaller branch of the Chahamanas/Chauhans at [[Chahamanas of Naddula\|Naddula]] (950-1197). 

While the Naddula branch was loyal to the mainline in 1192's [[Second Battle of Tarain (1192)\|Second Battle of Tarain (1192)]] and later vassalised themselves with the [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] and mostly ended in the [[Battle of Kasahrada (1197)\|Battle of Kasahrada (1197)]] with very few survivors (who survived by either agreeing to be vassals or survived by seeking asylum in Jalor).

The branch of [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Chahamanas of Jalor]] ruled for much longer since it had agreed to become a vassal of the new [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] fairly soon in the 1190s without subsequent war but later went independent when weak rulers were in power and Jalor was ruled by [[Udaysimha\|Udaysimha]]. 

After the Delhi Sultanate had captured the region of [[Chahamanas of Naddula\|Chahamanas of Naddula]] after winning in the [[Battle of Kasahrada (1197)\|Battle of Kasahrada (1197)]], the Jalor Chauhans recaptured it sometime during the reign of Jalor King [[Udaysimha\|Udaysimha]] under the nose of Delhi's Sultan [[Aram Shah\|Aram Shah]].

Eventually [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Chahamanas of Jalor]] were taken out by [[Alauddin Khilji\|Alauddin Khilji]] in the [[Siege of Jalor (1311)\|Siege of Jalor (1311)]].

### Chauhan Rajputs Today
Today Chauhan Rajputs have subclans such as the Hada, Bhadauria, Deora among others although the main clan is still alive and kicking. They still survive and have a thriving clan inside the Rajput community in the modern day which actively participates in contemporary democratic politics & can be found both in the cities and villages.

Chauhan Families are also found as far as Bihar, Odisha and other historical territories of the famous [[Pala Dynasty of Bengal\|Pāla Dynasty of Bengal]] since there was vassalisation and inter-marriages with Rajput Clans after [[Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj\|Tripartite Wars]], it is not too far-fetched to think that there were Rajput soldiers stationed there or land grants for Rajputs made in the region.

Even to this day the "Chauhan" surname is very easily spotted in the Rajput community. I had 2 friends in College from this very Rajput Clan/Gotra and they were both on role numbers directly adjacent to mine (I was sandwiched between them) and I often sat with them on exams.
