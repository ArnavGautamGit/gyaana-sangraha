# Advice for Intellectual Growth
> I would like to leave behind as advice for my child/children regardless of how much time I get to spend with them talking about such intellectual topics. Children tend to quickly go from being too dumb for understanding topics like these all the way to growing into independent beings who do not talk to you about such topics despite being smart enough to understand these topics.

The following are less like advice and framed more similar to cold hard truths. I might do a series of notes on "Advice I want to leave behind for my kids" or "Hard Truths of Life" to cover more topics than before.

### What is Intellectual Growth?
To receive advice on any topic, one needs an understanding of said topic. 
Similarly, to understand any topic one needs to define it.
The phrase "Intellectual Growth" is made of two words: intellect & growth.

The word Intellect comes from Intelligence whose exact definition (apart from the general bookish definition given in [[Cognitive Intelligence\|these notes]]) is largely dependent on perspective due to there being no consensus on what even exactly constitutes intelligence & what ratio of constituents makes one more intelligent than the other. 

On the other hand, growth is something that is very clearly defined; it is the increase of any metric from a previous level to the next. Growth is often measured in decimals/fractions or in percentage but it entirely depends on the party that is measuring the growth.

### Absolute vs Perceived Growth
Intellectual Growth is dependent upon the baseline intellect of the person along with the direction and rate of growth. While the Direction of growth us defined as the area which the person is studying/learning about, the Rate of growth is affected by many things such as motivation, discipline, nature among other uncontrollable factors such as the environment a person has grown up in & continues to live in. 

Often times, people think that they are stuck in life, what they fail to understand is that absolute growth & perceived growth are two different things...
Absolute Growth is the one where you are comparing where you have reached now with where you were earlier, Perceived Growth is the one where you estimate how you much you must have grown. 

It is correct to assume that Perceived Growth is more important for material success like getting a better job or higher pay since it is inherently comparitive in nature & ranks you among other humans, it is equally important to keep in mind that this measure invites comparison and most-likely the people feeling stuck in life compare themselves with their peers who they have met due to being in the same school, same class, same after-school tuition or something similar along those lines which acts as an anchor to connect them with the particular friend who seems to be ahead or gotten ahead i.e., the two people that have the same baselines may not have the same rate or direction of growth.

### Growth via Grinding
In situations where Perceived Growth or comparison with your peers make you feel bad about youself or your situation it is commonly advised to remind where you have come from i.e., keep in mind the Absolute Growth you have embarked upon throughout the journey. I feel this advice is incomplete because you must also see the direction in which you were trying to go versus the direction you are now going or forced to go may need to overcome the [[Inertia\|Inertia]] of your single-focused growth until now. 
For Example: If you were earlier studying engineering but now want to instead go into public service via [[UPSC Civil Services Exam\|UPSC Civil Service Exam]] then you have to overcome the inertia of studying a polar opposite subject like engineering compared to a subject like Political Science for 4 years along with 2 years from 11th to 12th grades in school.

This phase of fighting against Growth Inertia is called a "grind" and the mindset required to grind is called the "grindset". At the end of the day, grind is a fancier term for "perseverance". The ability to grind & persevere is an important skill in life which comes in handy everytime life is not going perfectly to plan.

### You Cannot Have it All
> Full note: [[Four Burner Theory of Success\|Four Burner Theory of Success]]

Imagine yourselves as a cooking stove with 4 burners which are work, relationships, health and hobbies. You have limited fuel but if all 4 burners will go full power, you will exhaust all the fuel really rapidly leaving you burnt out but if you can regulate the burners, turning few of them down or completely off while others go full power, you can regulate your energy flow better.

Problems arise in the theory when you realise work cannot be off, it can only be turned down slightly at most, hence you have to keep rotating among the others if you want to have it all.
