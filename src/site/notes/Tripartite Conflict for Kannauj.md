# Tripartite Conflict for Kannauj
> Also called as "Kannauj Wars" and "Tripartite Struggle" was a series of wars fought between the three empires: [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]], [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]] and [[Pala Dynasty of Bengal\|Pala Dynasty of Bengal]] with all three of them trying to gain control over Kannauj by defeating the [[Ayudhas of Kannauj\|Ayudhas]] who ruled the Kingdom of Kannauj at the time.

These were fought along a period from around 785 AD to 815 AD for about 30 years. It was a heavy struggle over the city and surrounding region for Kannauj over the power vacuum left behind after the collapse of [[Pushyabhuti Dynasty\|Pushyabhuti Dynasty]] and the rule eventually had fallen into the hands of the weakening [[Ayudhas of Kannauj\|Ayudhas of Kannauj]].

### Prelude
Regions of present Gujarat and Rajasthan were controlled by the Gurjara-Pratihara while Rashtrakutas controlled much of Deccan. Palas controlled Bengal and Bihar. At the same time the regions of Malwa, in present-day MP and all of present-day UP was an absolute warzone.

[[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]] was made up of multiple royal/noble families who later took on the identity of "Rajputs" including the [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandela Rajputs]], [[Parmar Rajputs\|Parmar Rajputs]] among other families. They already had scores to settle with [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]] after [[Dāntidurga\|Dāntidurga]] had defeated their founder [[Nagabhata I\|Nagabhata I]] as mentioned in the Dashavatara Temple Inscription and Sanjan Inscriptions erected by [[Dāntidurga\|Dāntidurga]] and [[Amoghavarsha I\|Amoghavarsha I]] respectively. Currently ruled by [[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]], the grand-nephew of [[Nagabhata I\|Nagabhata I]], the Pratihara Empire has annexed Gurjaradesha in northern and eastern Gujarat and transformed into a much bigger empire with others like [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] and [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chahamanas of Shakambari]] choosing to ally themselves with the Pratiharas as well.

### First Tripartite War (785 AD to 795 AD)
Triggered by the [[Ayudhas of Kannauj\|Ayudhas of Kannauj]] declaring [[Indrāyudha\|Indrāyudha]] as their new ruler, it started a frantic chase into capturing the north while it was weak, the Pratiharas struck first with [[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]] being successful in defeating the new king. 

Overwhelmed by his swift and decisive victory, Vatsaraja moved towards Bengal. He sent his vassal and King of Shakambari (Sambhar/Ajmer) [[Durlabharaja I Chahamana\|Durlabharaja I Chahamana]] who was one of the [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chahamanas of Shakambari]] to attack Bengal on his behest.

[[Dharmapāla\|Dharmapāla]] lost to Durlabha but asked [[Dhruva Dharavarsha\|Dhruva Dharavarsha]] of [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]] to help him out. The Technicality of the agreement was that he accepts the supremacy of the [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]] and becomes a vassal of his but in exchange Rashtrakutas (who were keeping an eye on all of this) would ambush [[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]]'s forces as they return with the spoils of war.

[[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]] somehow made it out alive and retreated to the Maru Desert (now Thar Desert) while [[Dhruva Dharavarsha\|Dhruva Dharavarsha]] moved to attack and defeat [[Dharmapāla\|Dharmapāla]] but had no intention of creating a permanent presence in the North so he returned back to the South.

### Dharmapāla's invasion of Kannauj (791 AD)
[[Dharmapāla\|Dharmapāla]] was not ready to team up with someone who bashed him ([[Pratihara Empire\|Gurjaras]]) just to bash the party who slapped the shit out of him ([[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakutas]]) after the first one ([[Pratihara Empire\|Gurjaras]]) was done.

[[Dharmapāla\|Dharmapāla]] decided to attack Kannauj in 791 when [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]] was not expecting it and [[Dhruva Dharavarsha\|Dhruva Dharavarsha]]'s forces returned home down south. Due to the element of surprise, he won and installed his vassal and [[Indrāyudha\|Indrāyudha]]'s brother [[Chakrayudha\|Chakrayudha]] as the new king. Many surrounding smaller nations like Kuru (Delhi), Matsya (Jaipur), Madra (East Punjab) and Yavana among a few others came to the coronation ceremony. 

It is said that Gurjara-Pratihara was on a decline here due to Matsya (present-day Jaipur & North-East Rajasthan) were moving towards the Pālas even going to Chakrayudha's coronation. During this time between 791 AD and 795 AD, [[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]] also passed away. The [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakutas]] also got a new king [[Govinda III\|Govinda III]] (son of [[Dhruva Dharavarsha\|Dhruva Dharavarsha]]) in 793 AD.

### Govinda III's Northward Campaign (798 AD to 800 AD)
When Pratihara-Emperor [[Vatsaraja\|Vatsaraja]] was succeeded by his son [[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] in 795 AD, before the Pratiharas could get the chance to regain balance after repeated setbacks, the new Rashtrakuta Emperor [[Govinda III\|Govinda III]] decided to attack them and try to finish them off for good.

Govinda captured Malwa after securing the Vindhya hill passes by attacking via Bhopal and Jhansi while defeating [[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] near Gwalior. Since both [[Dharmapāla\|Dharmapāla]] and his appointed vassal [[Chakrayudha\|Chakrayudha]] both submitted, there was no reason for [[Govinda III\|Govinda III]] to continue his campaign. 

### Gurjara-Gauda Wars (816 AD)
After [[Govinda III\|Govinda III]] passed away in 814 AD, [[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] and [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]] spent no extra time before attacking Kannauj while the [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]] collapsed under their own weight. They attacked and ended the control of the [[Ayudhas of Kannauj\|Ayudha Dynasty]] over Kannauj.

[[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] declares Kannauj as the Pratiharan capital and along with [[Pratiharas of Jodhpur\|Pratiharas of Jodhpur]], [[Chaulukyas of Saurashtra\|Chaulukyas of Saurashtra]] and [[Guhilas of Medapata\|Guhilas of Medapata]] thoroughly defeated whatever army that [[Dharmapāla\|Dharmapāla]] could muster in the battle known as the "Battle of Monghyr" (Monghyr is the old name of the city of Munger).

[[Nagabhata II\|Nagabhata II]] now took on the title of Paramabhattaraka (meaning "All-Powerful") and ended the tripartite struggle permanently.

### Aftermath
This conflict made sure all three emerging empires exhaust themselves in war but eventually all those families that identified with the "Rajput" identity and submitted to the Pratiharas formed the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]] and the North-Western kingdoms under the confederacy began to focus on the evolving situation in the western border with [[Islam\|Islam]] knocking on the door. The Rajput families were able to rebuild themselves rather quickly with all the revenue flowing from the rich [[North Indian Plains\|North Indian Plains]]. Culturally, the Rajputs in the aftermath of these wars began constructing monuments such as the [[Khajuraho Group of Temples\|Khajuraho Group of Temples]] made by the [[Chandela Rajputs\|Chandela Rajputs]] (who gained prominence and were rewarded for the loyalty after supporting the Pratiharas). 

The [[Pala Dynasty of Bengal\|Pāla Dynasty of Bengal]] did survive with [[Devapāla\|Devapāla]] as the new king with a few independent kingdoms along the buffer between the confederacy and Pāla territory.

While in the deccan, the [[Rashtrakuta Empire\|Rashtrakuta Empire]] collapsed after the death of [[Govinda III\|Govinda III]] amidst the resurgence of the [[Imperial Chola Dynasty\|Imperial Chola Dynasty]].
