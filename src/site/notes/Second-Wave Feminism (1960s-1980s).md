# Second-Wave Feminism (1960s-1980s)
> The Second Wave of Feminism which began from the mid-1960s and went until the end of the 1980s. It was spear-headed by authors like Kate Millet in the 1970s.

Second-Wave of Feminism is characterised by the politicisation of the female body, the female sexuality and family structures themselves. Feminists of this era framed the personal and private relationships into a power struggle of the ruler and ruled where men control the family via patriarchal tools such as marriage and family.

Feminists of this era questioned [[Monogamy\|Monogamy]] and ideas of women not wanting to be with one man all their life came to the fore. They questioned the idea of why women need to be monogamous.

### Kate Millet's Role in the movement
Since Kate Millet and her ideas listed in the book "Sexual Politics" released in the 1970s are considered to be the defining milestones of [[Feminism\|The Feminist Movement]] during this era, it is easy to understand the Feminist viewpoint if one understands Kate Millet's point of view.

Kate Millet equated Family with a social structure made by men or at least twisted by them for patriarchal control while ideas such as Love were considered political traps. Sexuality was often considered a power structure and a social class in itself similar to the view of [[Karl Marx\|Karl Marx]] where he defined the Industrialists being the rulers while the workers being the ruled. 
Kate just replaced Industrialists with men and workers with women.

Further Kate equated Dependence on Husbands with Enslavement to the men.
She said that being married means sharing property rights among other things. Many famous female celebrities like Neha Dhupia and Deepika Padukone equate adultery with quotes such as "It's my choice" i.e., monogamy is actively looked down upon.

She argues how women are always taught how to sit, how to laugh and how to eat in a dignified way (as if men are not taught to be gentleman). She conveniently forgets that these are called "values" - these are needed in the society to shape the moral compass of the children.

The Feminists like Kate Millet essentially politicised the bedroom of your house and your private relationship just because the men controlled laws. They did not demand for changes in laws, most changes had to begin from the home and family spaces. While there were needed changes such as Domestic Violence and Marital Rape but infiltrating and politicising the private spaces between men and women instead of pushing for laws is where this movement lost me since they resorted to Emotional Blackmail and witholding sex to get their way.

Even in India, vermillion (also called Sindoor) is often questioned and Karva Chauth is questioned just because of the ideas propagated in this wave of Feminism. Where did they get this idea from? The people who they got this from, where did they get it from? The root lies here in the Second Wave of Feminism.
