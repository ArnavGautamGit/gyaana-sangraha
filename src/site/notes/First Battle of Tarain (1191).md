# First Battle of Tarain (1191)
> First large-scale battle fought between the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rājpūts]] led by [[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] against the [[Ghurid Empire\|Ghurid Empire]] led by [[Mohammad Ghori\|Mohammad Ghori]].

After [[Mohammad Ghori\|Mohammad Ghori]] had taken control of most of the regions that the [[Ghaznavid Empire\|Ghaznavid Empire]] held and had almost completely replaced the eastern 35-40% of them by the next century comes to an end with the [[Seljuk Empire\|Seljuk Empire]] taking the rest. He quickly set his eyes on a new swath of land he could conquer. It was important to note that while the western side of his empire was bordered by the [[Seljuk Empire\|Seljuks]] he had nowhere to go.

### Buildup to the War
Seeing Mohammad of Ghor as the new threat emerging, Prithviraj Chauhan had dealt with him a lot of times before (in the sense of fending off his smaller raids) and according to folklore and some contemporary sources, it is said that Prithviraj had stopped 17 Ghurid raids.

The Chauhans were now the dominant power in the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]] and Prithviraj was adored due to his charisma and the power he commanded at the young age of 23 with presumably many years ahead of him.

### The Rājpūts defeat the Afghans
Mohammad Ghori's massive [[Ghurid Empire\|Ghurid]] army faced the highly skilled and equally large army of the [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]]. The higher skill and terrain-knowledge ended up giving the Rajputs much more of an advantage than the Afghans expected.

It is said that Mohammad of Ghor tried to hold a fort near Karnal in Tarain but was driven out and even wounded in action but somehow got away with the help of his general and [[Mamluk\|Mamluk]] slave named [[Qutub ud-Din Aibak\|Qutub ud-Din Aibak]].

### Aftermath
[[Prithviraja III Chauhan\|Prithviraja III Chauhan]] belonged to the [[Chahamanas of Shakambari\|Chahamanas of Shakambari]] branch of the [[Chauhan Rajputs\|Chauhan Rajputs]] and had fallen in love with a rajput princess of another kingdom whose father Jaichand was against their marriage. This caused political troubles in garnering support from the rest of the Rajputana Clans but we will cover that in the [[Second Battle of Tarain (1192)\|Second Battle of Tarain (1192)]] that happened next year when [[Mohammad Ghori\|Mohammad Ghori]] decided to face Prithviraj head-on.
