# Home Solar Setup
> With what all I have studied in elective subjects like [[Green Energy & Climate Modelling\|Green Energy & Climate Modelling]] and other similar subjects in my [[Academics\|My Academic Life]] which provide the theoretical backdrop along with some YouTube videos that provide for practical product knowledge of market realities for building a setup. 

Home Solar Setups are of different kinds and may change depending upon whether you want a fixed setup or a portable one. Sometimes it may depend on whether you want a Solar Connection for the ENTIRE house or for a specific few appliances.

There are a few ways of doing things, 
1. You could get permanent Solar Power Plant installed from a good company in their country (India for me) if there is a good company that exists in the space. They would add a few kilowatts of solar panels often on raised structures affixed to their roof so that there is a permanent addition of Solar Power.
2. You could get a portable Power Station along with compatible Solar Panels for a good price as well. Although larger power stations with higher capacity come with heavier weight and bigger price which beyond a point becomes a situation where you could get a fixed setup for your full home.

Let's Discuss them both one by one, starting with the portable option first.

## Portable Power Stations w/ Solar Panels
Solar Panels (of around 100 to 200 Watt output) are the cheaper expense where most Panels sold in India cost around ₹10,000 INR ($100 USD) and thereabouts as of May 2026. Most Solar Panels come with an MC4 port for connecting your solar panels.

Power Stations that are portable and small enough for a input power of around 100 Watts to 200 Watts of Solar Charging sell for around ₹50,000 INR (or about $500 USD) as of 2026. These prices may increase (and most likely will) as time goes on - all thanks to [[Inflation\|Inflation]].

### Compatibility
Compatibility is the main difference when choosing your Power Station i.e., you need to match the input max Wattage, Amperage and Voltage of the power station with the maximum wattage, [[Open Circuit Voltage\|Open Circuit Voltage]] and [[Short Circuit Current\|Short Circuit Current]] of the Solar Panels. 

> [!tip] TIP: Keep a gap between Panel's Voltage & Power Station's Voltage Limits
> It is advised for the Open Circuit Voltage of the panel to be 20% less than the upper limit of whatever the power station can handle because in the winter, there is a surge in Voltage produced by the Panels. If you live at a location where there is extreme winter then you should probably go with a 25% gap.

For the power station, the limits are always listed on the unit's back, bottom or other markings near the input ports. You may need to check the user manual or online product page on the brand's website or Amazon page and if all else fails, a Youtube Product Review is always great. While for solar panels, you should scroll down to check the stats in the product specification section of the product page, user manuals or YouTube Product Reviews. 

Certain Power Stations allow for overpanneling (exceeding Wattage Limits) but it is best to check user manuals or email the brand's support email anyway.

Other than the numbers you must also check the Solar Charging Port that the Power Stations comes with. Although cables are generally shipped unless looking at budget units (whose prices I listed above). Solar Charging Ports are of many types, I am attaching screenshots below for each type. Now all one has to do is to get a cable that converts whatever of these ports are on the Power Station to the MC4 Connectors. This is how their Power Stations will receive charge. Athough most power stations can also charge from the Grid - sometimes even from your car (but that would incur Petrol Costs or more charging costs).

### Some Tips
> [!tip] TIP: Personal Power Station Recommendation
> If I were to buy a good combo of Solar Panel and Power Station then they would be a **Dabbsson 600L power station** which has a **Wattage Limit of 200 Watts** while the **Voltage Limit is 12-30V** and **Amperage Limit is 13A**. it is to be noted that while the Wattage Limit can be exceeded, the voltage and amperage limits cannot be exceeded without the power station catching fire. Although this unit does NOT come with a Solar Charging Cable, it comes with the most common XT60 port for which cables are cheap & easy to find.

> [!tip] TIP: Buy a short cable for MC4 to Solar Port (whichever one you have)
> If your plan is to have a long cable, the best idea is to get a longer MC4 to MC4 extension cable and then a short MC4 to Solar Port charging cable so later you can upgrade to a new power station (which may have a different Solar Port) or upgrade to bigger panels.
