# Kamran Mirza
> Kamran was the son of [[Babur\|Babur]]. He was the sibling of [[Mohammad Askari Mirza\|Mohammad Askari Mirza]] being born from the same mother but was the half-brother of [[Humayun\|Humayun]] and Babur's other children.

Kamran was handed the governorship (Dīwānī) of Kabul before his father's death and later after Babur died, the new [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal]] Emperor, [[Humayun\|Humayun]] extended his rights to also include the city of Kandahar and surrounding regions. 

### Attempt at Sovereignity
When Humayun became the Emperor, as an endearing act he minted coins in the Afghan region to have both the names of Humayun and Kamran minted on them but Kamran secretly also started issuing coins with only his name on them with the title Badshāh (Emperor).

When Kamran started to reduce his involvement in Mughal raids, it was largely ignored thinking that Afghanistan is a difficult region to rule anyway since the climate is arid, being on the edge of the kingdom there is always a threat against raids and attacks from foreign powers and there is a lot to manage. Humayun was still being the considerate half-brother letting Kamran "do his thing" in the region.

Kamran later even tried to claim the title of Badshah of Afghanistan and did so secretly. He was able to successfully either hide his motives or offer limited support like in the campaign known as the [[Siege of Chunar (1531)\|Siege of Chunar (1531)]].

By the 1540s, when [[Sher Shah Suri\|Sher Shah Suri]] was on the rise, Kamran refused to support Humayun in an indirect support to the new rising power and eventually even striking his own deals with Suri after [[Battle of Kannauj (1540)\|Battle of Kannauj (1540)]] — succesfully playing his hand in getting his half-brother exiled to Persia in 1543.

### Struggle for Legitimacy and eventual Capture
Once Humayun returned from Persia, he arrived at Kabul in 1545, just two years later and deposed Kamran from Kabul. It was said that at some point Kamran must have controlled Lahore so he could have commissioned the construction of "Kamran ki Baarah-dari", a weird 12-archdoor gazebo-like structure at the banks of the river Ravi in Lahore.

Until 1553, Kamran struggled for legitimacy and colluded with various Afghan Nobles to continue to raise rebellions and act against the state. He was captured and blinded in 1553 despite Humayun's court asking for an execution because he was instructed by their father Babur (before dying) to be kind to Kamran. The only request from Kamran's end was for the executioners to not push his knees and he is described to have taken the pain well muttering prayers among other things.

Kamran was also allowed to go to the [[Hajj Pilgrimage\|Hajj Pilgrimage]] where he passed away in October 1557.
