# Ghaznavid Raid of Mathura
In the year 1018 AD, [[Mahmud of Ghazni\|Mahmud of Ghazni]] raided the city of Mathura in India with a massive army and looted the people's homes snd destroyed countless temples and burnt the city to the ground.

After this raid Ghaznavid Empire moved towards [[Ghaznavid Raid of Kannauj\|Ghaznavid Raid of Kannauj]] since it was the capital of the rapidly declining [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]].

The soldiers of [[Ghaznavid Empire\|Ghaznavid Empire]] led by Mahmud is said to have brought back enormous amounts of Gold and Silver which was given exceptionally glowing descriptions in the works of contemporary historians such as [[Ferdowsi\|Ferdowsi]] and [[Abul-Fazl Beyhaqi\|Abul-Fazl Beyhaqi]] in their works such as "Shāhnāmā" (also spelled as Shahnameh) although that treasure also included the loot from [[Ghaznavid Raid of Somnath\|Raid of Somnath]] and [[Ghaznavid Raid of Kannauj\|Raid of Kannauj]].
