# Abu Nasir Mohammad Hindal Mirza
> Born as the fourth son of [[Babur\|Babur]] and the half-brother to [[Humayun\|Humayun]], [[Kamran Mirza\|Kamran]] and [[Mohammad Askari Mirza\|Askari]]. He was named "Hindal" due to him being born right before Babur started his journey towards India.

He was handed the governorship of the regions of Alwar and Mewat and later [[Humayun\|Humayun]] handed him Malwa and Ghazni as Jagir (Ancestral Land Property).

While Alwar was Babur's favourite retreat it held little strategic significance and all his properties were spread apart across multiple regions. Alwar in North Western India and while Mewat was closeby in North India but separated by quite some land, Malwa is almost a 1000 kilometres away in Central India and Ghazni was a city in Iran.

When [[Humayun\|Humayun]] returned from Persia after exile and re-established the Mughal Supremacy across India, Hindal held the governing rights to over 30% (capprox. 1/3rd) of the territory controlled by Mughals.
