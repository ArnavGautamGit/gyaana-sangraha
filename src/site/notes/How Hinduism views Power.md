# How Hinduism views Power
> It is a repeating theme in [[Hinduism\|Hinduism]] that the Gods particularly [[Lord Shiva\|Lord Shiva]] often bless the demons ([[Rakshasa\|Rakshasa]] and [[Asura\|Asura]]) and then when they abuse the power they received, the Gods do not intervene until the abuse of his power reaches its Zenith.

Contrary to many memes and criticism by Indian followers of [[Islam\|Islam]]
It is not that the Gods are blind to the fact that the person asking the boon has been showing the signs of abusing power and neither do the Gods enjoy the suffering of humans in some twisted sadistic way nor are the Gods handing out boons in their folly. 

### Power Requires Sacrifice
It is reflective of the sentiment that while those driven by goodness debate with themselves whether they should make the necessary sacrifices to get power, those driven by blind ambition and emotion, possibly a radical and evil ideology are more than willing to sacrifice anything it takes to reach the desired level of power so they can execute their plans and fulfill their unfulfilled desires. 

In essecne, it can be summarised as: "While goodness thinks, evil acts."

Many Hindus themselves do not understand that Power rarely corrupts but often only reveals who the person truly is i.e., Power only brings to the fore the true intentions and reasonings behind their ideology. Once all their plans reach fruition and their true nature is revealed. Their actions, intentions and ideology are then analysed on the scales of [[Dharma (धर्म)\|Dharma (धर्म)]]. 
It is only then — that the Gods take action to rid the Mortal Realm of the Adharmi.

### If the Gods know everything, why wait until Chaos Reigns?
Many would ask if the Gods know everything then they must also know the true plans deep inside the person's mind and whether the person asking for the boon will use the new powers for good or whether they will abuse it. If they know that the particular person will abuse the power then why do the Gods not kill the person right there?

It is because the person asking for a boon comes as a devotee who may or may not have a change of heart later, moreover killing them now would make it seem like a cold-blooded killing in Authoritarian Bloodlust. Evidence is needed for Transparent Justice and Trust.

Once the world sees the true intentions of the person asking for the boon, the regular populace can make their own decision. Sometimes many who have received a boon have done many benevolent things. For instance, [[Prahlad\|Prahlad]] was an [[Asura\|Asura]], so was [[Maharja Bali\|Maharja Bali]] but they were both benevolent and kind. Rishi Markandeya received the boon of immortality and never abused it despite all the flaws of being Human.
