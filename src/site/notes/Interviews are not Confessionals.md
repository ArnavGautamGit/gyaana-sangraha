# Interviews are not Confessionals
> Interviews are not confession booths where you owe the HR all your trauma and absolute truths that you went through in life or the sheer efforts you went through to get here.

For example, when the interviewer asks you to tell them something about yourselves they do not JUST want to know your degrees and the institutions you did them from... 

Your job is not to tell them something that is already on the resume, your job is to treat this as a real conversation between people that they want to keep listening and engaging in. Your Objective is to make yourself memorable and the conversation enjoyable so they prefer hiring you because they would prefer working with a human rather than a robot. 

> [!cite] After your Basic Introduction of your name & degree say something that makes you unique
> YOU: "For a few years I do well in my client-facing environments since I slow conversations down instead of rushing to note down everything. I focus on understanding the system from their point of view while others may want to just get the requirements quickly and be done.. This is why I think I was firmly within the top 10 percent of workers after the end of each month. What people take for granted about me is that I may not look flashy on paper but once a conversation begins I am hard to compete with since I believe good Communication skills, Empathy and Agreeableness to be my biggest strengths in life followed by Conscientousness and Openness to new Experiences.

Here are a few other examples on how to deal with the most popular questions about your resume and work experience that you can use to sound like a better candidate.


> [!cite] Explaining a Gap on your Resume
> Interviewer: "Could you please explain the X-month-gap on your Resume?"
> You: "I took a planned sabbatical to upskill re-allign on my career trajectory."

> [!cite] Work Experience at a Previous Company
> Interviewer: "Why are you looking to leave your current role?"
> You: "I have maxed out my growth potential there and I am looking for a dynamic challenge."

> [!cite] Question on Future Plans
> Interviewer: "Where do you see yourself in the next XYZ years?"
> You: "I see myself stepping into a more senior leadership position in this company."

You know you need to keep your options open for a 20% bump in pay or better opportunities to acquire more skills but the interviewer does not need to be extended this courtesy. Interviews are a negotiation, not a confessional.
