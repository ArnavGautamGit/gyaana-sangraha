# Humayun
> Born as "Nasir-al-Din Mohammad" but better known ny regnal name "Humayun", he was the Second Emperor of the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Empire]] being the second child and eldest son after his father [[Babur\|Babur]] died. The first child Fakhr-un-Nissa died as an infant.

Humayun was the Mughal Emperor who was not that favoured by the court, while Babur was alive there were constant (unsuccessful) attempts to make Babur announce his cousin as the new successor instead of Humayun (despite being Babur's eldest surviving child)

### No Support from his Brothers
Humayun was never favoured as king by his brothers either. There was definitely something in him that nobody ever truly liked the guy. [[Kamran Mirza\|Kamran Mirza]] declared himself Independent and even [[Mohammad Askari Mirza\|Askari Mirza]] did not give much of a shit about Humayun. Some may argue that Askari did not give much of a shit about the throne either. 

The only brother that supported Humayun was [[Abu Nasir Mohammad Hindal Mirza\|Hindal Mirza]]. He was there on most campaigns although he was quite young and could not fight as much due to being a teenager.

### Major Rivals and Emperors
[[Humayun\|Humayun]] had two major rivals for his kingdoms: 
To the southwest, he had Sultan-i-Gujarat [[Qutub ud-Din Bahadur Shah\|Qutub ud-Din Bahadur Shah]] while to the East along the river Ganga, he had [[Sher Shah Suri\|Sher Shah Suri]] controlling most of Bihar and trying to move westward into attacking him. 

Both of his rivals gained territory over the first 5 years of his reign from 1530 to 1535 AD.
Although Bahadur Shah was busy with sporadic conflicts with the Portuguese for control over Central India.

### First Campaign against Bahadur Shah
Humayun planned his first campaign to be against [[Sher Shah Suri\|Sher Shah Suri]] for full control of the Ganga River Basin but the campaign had to be urgently abandoned since [[Qutub ud-Din Bahadur Shah\|Bahadur Shah]] had attacked and annexed Malwa - one of the regions given to [[Abu Nasir Mohammad Hindal Mirza\|Hindal Mirza]] as governor. Hindal had arrived in Agra to plan the campaign while the attack took place.

Throughout the time while [[Qutub ud-Din Bahadur Shah\|Bahadur Shah]] was focused on the sporadic conflicts with the Portuguese in 1535, Humayun's forces stormed Malwa and Gujarat. This storming forced Bahadur Shah to retreat and reconcile with the Portuguese. In a revolt next year in 1536 AD, Sultan Bahadur Shah recaptured all of Gujarat.

### Tussle with Sher Shah Suri
Despite many attempts from Humayun, he could never defeat [[Sher Shah Suri\|Sher Shah Suri]] in the first round of wars in India because repeated betrayals from [[Kamran Mirza\|Kamran Mirza]] and even his own supportive brother [[Abu Nasir Mohammad Hindal Mirza\|Hindal Mirza]] had tried to usurp his throne and acted against him.

[[Sher Shah Suri\|Sher Shah Suri]] soon became the Afghan who vanquished the Chagtai [[Mughal Empire\|Mughals]] from India after Humayun's defeat at the [[Battle of Kannauj (1540)\|Battle of Kannauj (1540)]] and susbsequent usurpation of his throne by Hindal in 1541 forced Humayun to leave. 

### Return to India, Death and Legacy
When Humayun came back towards in India, he first dealt with [[Kamran Mirza\|Kamran Mirza]] at Kabul in 1545 before moving into India. Armed with support from the [[Safavid Empire of Persia\|Safavid Empire of Persia]] there was no stopping Humayun this time.

After Humayun restablished control in India, he rapidly expanded his empire to leave a large legacy for his son [[Akbar\|Akbar]] to inherit.

Humayun later died when he slipped and fell down the stairs of his Palace in Delhi just 6 months of finally settling in and enjoying his life around Delhi.
