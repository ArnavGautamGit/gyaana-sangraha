# Office Politics & How to Handle it
> Office Politics is defined as equating every move you make be as measured as a move made in game of [[Compendiums/Chess Compendium/Chess\|Chess]] (good chess players measure their moves). These moves may be built and geared towards faster promotions, or getting someone else kicked out of the way. It is essentially scheming your way to the top alongside hardwork.

Everyone hates politics at the office unless your office is wholesome enough that the phrase means discussing who you're going to vote for in the upcoming elections - which is healthy in a democratic country but if your office is like that then what are ***you*** doing ***here***? Curiosity? Curiosity is always welcome hehe. 

## How Office Politics often begins
I have been on the receiving end of Office Politics, this is the primary reason I wanted to write this note as an explainer on how to handle such events. I have always seen two primary causes for becoming "the target" of one, two or more employees. It is either envy or motives.

If it is envy, no matter what you do that cannot be fixed, if it is motives, then it probably is that you unintentionally came in the way of someone else's motives and plans of promotion or being the only person in the project team so that they can have autonomy but since you joined, they lost autonomy as there is a second party that may or may not be alligned.

Well Envy and Personal Motives are one thing, but you cannot avoid office politics if you are being a "yes man" either. 
1. You will be seen as a fishy person who is somehow always "too nice". People will think there is something up with you that you are not sharing or something.
2. You will hurt your own productivity and autonomy since your lack of refusal will make you seem as a weak and overtly-complient person who can be taken for granted. You will be doing the work of other people while others chill on your work.

## HR is not YOUR Friend, they're the Company's Friend.
There are two kinds of companies on this planet, ones that have an HR Department and the others that do not. In case you are new to the workforce, HR stands for Human Resource because that is how companies see you regardless of whatever "we are a family" stuff they say to your face. 

Discussing the pros and cons of each type of corporation is for some other day, but for now get it through your heads that the HR's salary is paid for by the company. They are their to protect the *company* from any and all liability regarding human resource. This means that if you raise questions that are too *uncomfortable* for their tastes, they will start trying to get rid of YOU instead of acknowledging your points and changing their processes.

If you do not have an HR in your company then your manager or someone lese above him will do this work of protecting the company instead of a defined department. I will let you decide which is the worse option.

Regardless of whether your company has an HR or not, you always ***DOCUMENT EVERYTHING***.
Every major communication apart from "how's the weather" needs to be on email. As much of it as you can, get it on email - there is a trail that acts as evidence. If your company has a WhatsApp group then that counts just fine.

## Documenting Everything... but HOW to document?
Anyone that gets paid by the company is someone you need to be weary of. Never let your guard down because none of these people are your family and this is not your home - its your workplace where you either work or get kicked.

Documentation as referred to in the previous section does not refer to some note you made in Obsidian or Notion since that is a journal entry and hearsay. If you have screenshots of the emails or WhatsApp/Slack/Teams messages they sent you then use those and leverage them to your advantage. 

## Use the HR to your advantage
A lot of people interpret HRs to be like a Nanny at the workplace handling the crybaby employees which is not true. The HRs hate it if you try to do this shit and you will be treated like a target to kick out since you are irritating as hell.

HR will hate you if you come to them complaining, they cannot act on some random person's hearsay complaint anyway. Also never forget that the HR is not your friend (as stated above)

You approach the HR asking for TOOLS on how to resolve the problem. Everyone appreciates it when you ***REDUCE*** their burden instead of being ***THE BURDEN***.

> [!cite] What to say to the HR instead
> YOU: "I have this problem adversly affecting my productivity. I wished to know the tools to report the problem to relevant people. I have the documented evidence to support my case" 
> *follow this up with a brief description of the problem*

Never have this conversation behind any closed doors in person, always write an email from your official company email to the HR so that you can easily report such things.

### Can we have a quick chat?
This question is going to be posed by many HRs or colleagues across your entire career. As mentioned previously the quick chat is going to be behind closed doors with no trail, no evidence with absolutely no witnesses (that will support your case) and no protection. It is a classic case of building a case against where only they have the evidence, witnesses and proofs and even if you are not being kicked out, you are not in for a good time Whatever you say in the meeting will be used against you.

> [!cite] Before saying ANYTHING else, say these words:
> YOU: "I want to be cooperative and I will absolutely meet with you. Can you tell me the purpose of this meeting in advance so that I can prepare accordingly?"

Once you say these words, you need to access your work email from your phone or laptop immediately to type out the following email:

> [!cite] Subject: Regarding our Conversation just now in/near *(insert part of the office where you talked)*
> "As per our conversation I understand we are meeting on *{insert date}* regarding *{insert topic}*. Please let me know if there is anything else that I should know, bring or prepare for."

Now you have some trail and evidence that a meeting happened or is happening whoever asked you for the quick chat knows that:
1. There is a record and evidence of when the meeting happened & on which topic.
2. You have proven that you document all your conversations without showing them your files you may have documenting office stuff.
3. Whatever that conversation was about, you will come prepared and the surprise factor is gone.

### HR is not the snake, Your Manager is
Most of the times, it will be your manager who will most often have "quick chats" that are off the record. It will be your manager who will practically beg the company's HR to get rid of you from the company since he does not like something about you among the following options:

1. The quality of your work is not as good.
2. The speed of your work is not as fast.
3. You are perceived as being less of a hard worker.
4. You have "served your purpose" and now there is no need to keep you.
5. Your manager simply does not like you

Don't get me wrong there could be more than these 5 reasons since there are never enough reasons to not like someone but the HR just gets caught in the crossfire everytime while the real snake is your manager who tells his manager or the HR (either one) to get rid of you completely.
