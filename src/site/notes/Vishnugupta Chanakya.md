# Vishnugupta Chanakya
> A Professor of Economics & Political Economics at the [[Takshila University\|Takshila University]], he was also the Guru of the first emperor of [[Mauryan Empire\|Mauryan Empire]] & even served in his court for some time.

Vishnugupta Chanakya, was also called Vishnugupta Kautilya and also by shortened versions of both of these names "Chanakya" and "Kautilya" is an extremely important figure in the [[Mauryan Empire\|Mauryan Empire]] and [[Indian History\|Indian History]].

### Contributions
After being insulted in the royal court of Pataliputra by [[Dhana Nanda\|Dhana Nanda]], he decided to actually replace & remove the [[Nanda Empire\|Nanda Empire]] with a competant king who could attack & defeat the Greeks. You can read more about this story in the notes on [[Dhana Nanda\|Dhana Nanda]] & [[Nanda Empire\|Nanda Empire]]

Vishnugupta is also credited for the most incredible books on Politics, Governance, Economy and other topics of royal importance. Both the books [[Kautilya Arthashāstra\|Kautilya Arthashāstra]] & [[Chanakya Neeti\|Chanakya Neeti]] were written by him. His [[Saptānga Theory\|Saptānga Theory]] & [[Mandala Theory\|Mandala Theory]] use these books as sources.

It is said that Chanakya came to the aid of [[Chandragupta Maurya\|Chandragupta Maurya]] during the complications surrounding [[Bindusāra Maurya#Birth\|Bindusāra's Birth]].

### Legacy
The idea of Akhand Bhārata or Unified India which is capable of fighting against invaders still powers the contemporary Indian governments. Some of his texts are so fundamental that they still apply today.

Many regions, villages, localities within cities are named after him as "Chanakyapuri" and "Kautilya Nagar" among other such variations although the former is the most popular.
