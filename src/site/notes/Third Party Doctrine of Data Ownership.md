# Third Party Doctrine of Data Ownership
> Known more prominently as "Third Party Doctrine", it is the legally defined exclusion to the [[4th Ammendment of the Constitution of the USA\|4th Ammendment of the American Constitution]] which states that any person, group of persons lose the right to claim 4th Ammendment protections over any data that the person or group of persons has voluntarily given to third parties. 

This definition was needed to answer the question of what a reasonable expectation of privacy is & how the term "reasonable" is even defined. This definition worked well in the 1970s since you had only phone numbers and bank records as the only forms of 3rd party data.

In 2026, courts across the USA are still following the same definition even when everything on the internet is via a third party! From Gmail to Social Media - what all will you self-host and maintain alone? Intelligence Agencies like the [[Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)\|CIA]] and [[Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)\|FBI]] are abusing this to buy copies of the databases from big tech companies and social media companies to get all the tech since under this doctrine the userdata now belongs to Google, Meta etc instead of the users themselves.

### Warrants are not needed if the data is no longer yours
If you think that buying the databases entirely in one go makes no difference since the would still need a [[Legal Warrant\|Search Warrant]] to actually search the database? No! They call it a "query" and not a search despite clearly investigating on people using their databases.

### Private Agreement between user and third-party is nothing
In the USA, there is no point and no way that your agrement made between you and say Google Drive to store your photos is valid. It is never considered valid in the USA so whatever Privacy Policies they have are technically for the EU and India and the rest of the world.

### Proposed Fixes
[[Surveillance Accountability Act (SAA, 2026)\|Surveillance Accountability Act (SAA, 2026)]] is an Act proposed by Naomi Brockwell and the Ludlow Institute where the data will be treated as [[Bailment\|Bailment]] i.e., the data will belong the user and only be handled by said third party in accordance to the provisions set by the third party's. It will also require a [[Legal Warrant\|Search Warrant]] to search or query or start any kind of investigation. [[Subpeona Requests\|Subpeona Requests]] are no longer enough.
