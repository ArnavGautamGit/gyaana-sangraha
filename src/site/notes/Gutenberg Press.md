# Gutenberg Press
> It was the first Printing Press made by a man named Johannes Gutenberg during the years of the [[European Renaissance\|European Renaissance]] in the year 1440 AD (or 1496 in [[Vikram Samvat Calendar\|Vikram Samvat Calendar]]) .

Gutenberg Press was made with movable metal blocks, i.e., horizontal mirrored images of individual letters were carved on the blocks and individual blocks would need to be positioned in the order to form words, and special blank blocks needed to be used for spaces. 

These metal blocks would be fit in a frame and coloured with ink before the frame itself was placed on screw presses so that turning the screw would press these blocks painted with ink leaving a mirror image of whatever was on the block.

Since the image of the letter on the block was already mirrored, mirror image of a mirror image would be the original image itself.

### Recreation of the Original Gutenberg Press

### Legacy of the Printing Press
The Printing Press started a revolution in mass communication and many modern jobs such as Journalism, Writing and others are attributed to the Printing Press. The mass-availability of books with printing (about 3,600 books a year compared to 400 a year by hand-printing) sparked such a mass-availability of books that it sparked an absolute Literacy Revolution.

Literacy became easy and everyone started to become educated. The first book Gutenberg had printed was the "The Bible" and it is often called the Gutenberg Bible. It is ironic that the church itself was troubled by the rapid spread of ideas it was causing due to a printing explosion.
