# Great Gifts for Privacy Enthusiasts
> A small note on a list of gifts you can give to your known people around you if you know that they care about their [[Personal Online Privacy\|Personal Privacy]] and [[Cyber Security (CyberSec)\|security]] when they are online.
> 
> I have compiled this list of potential gifts to buy for them that they would love to have. These are also some gifts you may receive from them when they teach you about personal privacy.

Before we gift anything, we should keep in mind some best practices such as not compromising the privacy of the person receiving the gift, you should ask them before giving away their address to any service like Amazon or ship the product to your own house so that you can hand-deliver the gift. Also it is a good practice to at least tell the other party that they will be receiving the gift at XYZ date.

### Physical Security Keys (especially from [Yubico](https://www.amazon.in/dp/B0BVNPWPCN/ref=twister_B0CDNQTXPK?_encoding=UTF8))
[[Multi-Factor Authentication\|Multi-Factor Authentication]] is the best way to protect your account. Physical Keys are better than authenticator apps since the keys are mapped to the exact website, so keys would not work on fake websites. Moreover Authenticator Apps use a method called as "shared secrets" which is based on [[Symmetric Key Encryption\|Symmetric Key Encryption]] meaning that the same key is used on both sides if an attacker can get a code and have the exact time you generated the code on, they can rebuild your symmetric key (or "shared secret") which is used to create these timed OTP codes in the first place i.e., 2FA methods using authenticator codes is prone to [[Phishing\|Phishing]] but physical keys are phishing-proof. 

### USB Data Blockers
Cheap devices available on Amazon that can be used to block Data Transfer on any USB cable. The idea is that the cable will only be able to charge your devices and not be able to transfer or request data as long as the USB cable is routed through the data blocker.
You can get these blockers for both USB Type C and USB Type A.

### Phone Case with Camera Covers
Many phone cases are sold with a camera slider or camera cover - even though they are rarer than those covers that come without these covers, if you can find a cover with camera cover for the exact phone model, this can work well. It is best to tell them you are buying them a surprise gift if you want to ask for their phone model, it still keeps the surprise of what the actual gift truly is.

### Webcam Covers
Covers for their webcams will enhance their privacy regardless of if they use these gifted covers on phone or on laptops. In personal experience, a webcam cover helped me enhance my privacy on apps like snapchat which take 24x7 camera permissions.

### Brand New Flashdrives (not used ones or unofficial ones)
Everyone would like to have a new flashdrive of 16 or 32 GB. You can even ask them if they want flash storage in the form of SD or MicroSD cards instead... These could be used in their cameras, for storing Linux Distros or device backups! Trust me, your friend will love it as long as the drive is new & bought from an official retailer. try to buy from a good brand.
