# Feroz Shah Tughlaq
> Cousin of [[Mohammad bin Tughlaq\|Mohammad bin Tughlaq]] and the 17th [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] who tried to project himself as a staunch islamist to keep the Sultanate from fracturing into pieces.

He ruled from 1351 AD to 1388 AD.
Feroz failed to reintegrate [[Bengal Sultanate\|Bengal Sultanate]] twice during his reign & was seen as a weak military leader. This made him try tactics to project himself as a better Islamist leader than the competition so he does not end up assassinated.

### Institutional Changes to Jizya
Feroz wanted to impress the Ullema & the Turkic Nobles so he charged [[Jizya\|Jizya]] separately from the regular kharaj tax on agricultural & economic produce that was being charged since the times of [[Khilji Dynasty\|Khilji Dynasty]].

Subsequently, to seem like an even more staunch islamist, he imposed Jizya on Brahmins as well. This was never done before since brahmins do not contribute to economic activity anyway & live on donated alms. Now brahmins had to involve themselves with some kind of economic activity to sustain themselves - this is when Hinduism became slightly extractive and big donations gave access to VIP treatment. 

I am not trying to say that individual greed was never at play & some brahmin somewhere did not try to extort as much money he could from his clients but now since they have to pay Jizya & cannot survive on alms alone, it was incentivised for Brahmins en-masse to act in an extractive/extortive manner. 

The brahmins could not do ordinary jobs since they had been detached from economic activity for so long as monks & ascetics that they were out of touch with society, some tookup jobs like farming & dairy farming.

### Introduction of Tax on Irrigation Water
On most agricultural lands, the Irrigation Water used by a particular land owner started to be measured and taxed by a special tax known as [[Sharb Water Tax\|Sharb]]. This tax only applied if the irrigation water was sourced from canals made by the state.

While it is true new connections were made for irrigation of water & the state made new water canals into villages but at the same time, we have to see that the water canals built were built with the intent of extracting more [[Sharb Water Tax\|Sharb]] & [[Jizya\|Jizya]].
