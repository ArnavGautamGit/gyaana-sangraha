### Background & Prologue of the Movie
Directed by Aditya Dhar (who also directed [[Uri - The Surgical Strike\|Uri - The Surgical Strike]]) Dhurandhar is a great flick which shows a slice of an Indian spy's life in Pakistan. It does not fear from showing the sheer violence and gore that Pakistani society is built with. 

The movie starts with [[IC814 Kandahar Hijack\|IC814 Kandahar Hijack]] story where Ajay Sanyal (R. Madhavan) is negotiating with the terrorists. It is explained how the government of the day has bowed to external pressure from other [[State (United Nations)\|Nations]] along with internal pressure of relatives of the hijacked people staging a protest outside PM's residence.

KS Bhullar (based on [[AS Dulat\|AS Dulat]]), the ex-secretary of [[R&AW\|R&AW]] was also shown in the correct light - he was a man who presided over [[IC814 Kandahar Hijack\|IC814 Kandahar Hijack]] & yet tried to push for "cordial relations" with Pakistani Government denying their role as a state actor of terrorism against India. In the 2010s he even co-authored a book with the ISI chief of his time and has taken part in the [[Bharat Jodo Yatra\|Bharat Jodo Yatra]] of [[Rahul Gandhi\|Rahul Gandhi]]. In the movie, his character has a different name but the hairstyle and pitch of [[Aman Ki Asha\|Aman Ki Asha]] bullshit tells me exactly that it is him. 

### References to Pakistani Underground Culture (Bachabaazi)
The starting scene of the Babu Dakait gang member sniffing the testicles of Hamza/Ranveer showcasing how deep segregation between men & women can twist the people due to sexual frustration. It also acts as a stand-in reference to [[Bachabaazi\|Bachabaazi]].

There are also references to underground, illegal rave parties that the rich of Pakistan actually go to - none of these have permissions and Yalena is shown to attend a similar party fairly early in the film.

### Immersion & the Audience being the 3rd party in the frame
The movie is framed with immense immersion in mind. Closeups are frequent to reflect how humans would recall & remember faces separately from background context. Focal Length is kept such that the background is not blurred - similar to how the human eye sees things while the aperture and camera angles are very natural.

Characters are not afraid to look directly into the camera - greatest examples include the scene where Hamza first meets yelena on the political rally, where Yalena looks directly at the camera in a closeup thrice.

### Division into Chapters, Set Design in Thailand & Peak Details
Additionally, the movie is divided into chapters with text on the screen to add to the feeling that this is all a part of some mental or written notes of the characters - particularly the written notes of Hamza. Every night, Hamza still adds to his notes or changes them. Every major scene when Hamza is not on the table he can be seen around the table/conversation in the background, snooping... lurking like an actual spy.

It is complemented by immensely realistic and well-studied set designs which look like actual locations within the Lyari Town in Karachi. 
![Pasted image 20260519230228.png](/img/user/Vaulted%20Images/Dhurandhar/Pasted%20image%2020260519230228.png)

Even at the end of the film when Hamza is already at the hospital but does not get himself treated/bandaged despite bleeding profusely because it will show up that there is no medical records for him & he does not have a Pakistani National ID either.

### Overall Impression of the main protagonist
Overall the film really shows how Hamza/Ranveer is [[The Ideal Spy\|The Ideal Spy]] as he is quick-thinking, speaks sparingly, rehearsed and composed in every scene of the movie barring a few moments where he quickly regains composure - the way he uses the environment for his advantage during combat shows how well he was trained by Ajay Sanyal (R. Madhavan) himself. 

The diary in which he notes his targets and kills was given to him by Aalam Bhai (played by Gaurav Gera) after the [[26-11 Mumbai Terrorist Attack\|26-11 Mumbai Terrorist Attack]] scene. Leaving a written record on a diary is the only thing that is anti-Ideal in spying.

### Conclusion
The movie is easily one of the best franchises in Indian Movie History & nothing comes close by a longshot. The Baahubali Franchise comes close but it was based in fantasy and set in the medieval era when such exaggerated accounts of the feats achieved by kings & warriors was very common & period-accurate.

I never thought India was ready for such a grounded action-thriller-adventure based on true events and techniques used in real-world espionage. Although many people would say that the real Hamza Ali Mazaari in the Baloch gang of Lyari died in Operation Lyari, it is to be noted that Hamza in the movie is supposed to be the amalgamation of all the "unknown gunmen" that have gone into Pakistan to kill terrorists based in Pakistan and also their handlers from the [[Inter-Services Intelligence (ISI)\|Inter-Services Intelligence (ISI)]]. 

Everyone hoped for [[Dhurandhar Part 2 - The Revenge (2026)\|Dhurandhar Part 2 - The Revenge (2026)]] to also be this good and after watching that one as well I can say that our expectations were met quite handsomely. Check out that review in its own note (linked above).
