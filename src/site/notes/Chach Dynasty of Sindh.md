# Chach Dynasty of Sindh
> A Dynasty of [[Hinduism\|Hindu]] Brahmin Rulers in Sindh (which at the time also contained some parts of Punjab and Balochistan) who were mentioned in the [[Chāch Nāmā\|Chāch Nāmā]].

The first ruler of this dynasty was a Brahmin noble named [[Chach of Aror\|Chāch (चाच)]] who was handed the throne by the previous king for being a loyal nobleman who married the widowed Queen to ascend the throne. The previous king's son who ruled Chittor came to challenge his rule but was killed by deceit. It is believed that the previous king is Rai Sahasi II of the Rai Dynasty. We know little about the Rai Dynasty apart from the names of their kings.

Later, after [[Chach of Aror\|Chāch]] died his brother (Chandra/Chandar) ruled as regent for 8 years until Chach's son "Dahir" was of legal age to ascend the throne. Dahir later became King and was known as [[Raja Dahir\|Raja Dahir]].

### Chach connection of Rājpūts
Rajput rulers and many Rajput clans have rigins with the previous dynasty ruling Sindh before Chach, since the previous king of Sindh had a son who ruled Chittor. It is obvious that we can deduce how Rājpūt have Chach and Rai origins since many other Rajputana Clans have origins in the cavalry regiments of Sindh and went on to create many many states in the times of the [[Pratihara Empire\|Pratihara Empire]] and [[Rajputana Confederacy\|Rajputana Confederacy]].
