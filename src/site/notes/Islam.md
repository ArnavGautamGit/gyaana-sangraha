### Basic Background on the Faith/Religion
General consensus on how Islam formally began as a religion was when [[Prophet Muhammad\|Prophet Muhammad]] migrated from Mecca to Medina in the [[Hijrat (Great Migration)\|Hijrat (Great Migration)]] in 622 AD. This year marked the starting first year in the [[Hijri Calendar\|Hijri Calendar]].

When [[Prophet Muhammad\|Prophet Muhammad]] died in 632 AD (11th year in Hiijri Calendar), Islam was then soon led by a Caliph named [[Abū Bakr al-Saddiq\|Abū Bakr al-Saddiq]] who was a great friend and father-in-law of the prophet. This started the [[Rashidun Caliphate\|Rashidun Caliphate]] which was not accepted by all, especially the Iranian [[Shia Muslims\|Shia Muslims]] who rebelled against the Caliphate of Abū Bakr ([[Sunni Muslims\|Sunni Muslims]]) and fought the [[Ridda Wars\|Ridda Wars]] against them. Shias believed that Muhammad is not the last prophet while the Sunni's believed that Muhammad is the last & the Caliphate will be ruled in his name.

The Rashidun Caliphate later overthrown by the [[Ummayyad Caliphate\|Ummayyad Caliphate]] which was then overthrown by the [[Abassid Caliphate\|Abassid Caliphate]].
