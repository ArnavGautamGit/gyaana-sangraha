# Maitrakas of Saurashtra & Vallabhi
> A dynasty of rulers founded by Bhatārka who were previously governor and generals during the [[Gupta Empire\|Gupta Empire]], broke free and declared themselves independent after Guptas declined.

I will add more as I find out more but it is well known that their capital was the city of Vallabhi and they ruled from 475 AD to 776 AD. They were at the northern border of the [[Chalukya Dynasty\|Chalukyas]] at the time of the [[Chalukya-Pallava Wars\|Chalukya-Pallava Wars]].

At the North-Western Border of the Maitrakas, you would reach the [[Chach Dynasty of Sindh\|Chach Dynasty of Sindh]] as long as you stay off to the west of the Thar Desert.

### Slow Transition to Independent Titles
They moved slowly from vassals/generals to independent kingdom. Since the first two rulers (including the founder Bhatārka and his son "Dharasena I") used the title of "Senāpati" which means "General of the Army" until the third ruler [[Dronasimha\|Dronasimha]] used the title "Maharaja".

King Guhasena stopped using the term _Paramabhattaraka Padanudhyata_ along his name like his predecessors, which denotes the cessation of displaying of the nominal allegiance to the Gupta overlords. Guhasena was succeeded by his son Dharasena II, who was the first who used the title of _Mahadhiraja_. His son, the next ruler Śilāditya I Dharmāditya was described by [[Hiuen Tsang\|Hiuen Tsang]] in 640 AD as a "monarch of great administrative ability and of rare kindness and compassion". Śilāditya I was succeeded by his younger brother Kharagraha I.

Dharasena III married the daughter of [[Harshavardhan\|Harshavardhan]] (from the [[Pushyabhuti Dynasty\|Pushyabhuti Dynasty]]).
While they retained local autonomy they came under the rule of the Pushyabhuti Dynasty in sometime between the 640s and 670s...

### Coinage & Cultural Contributions
Their First few coins used [[Brāhmi Script\|Brāhmi Script]] and looked similar to coins of [[Western Kshatrapa\|Western Kshatrapa]]. Possibly to position themselves as being distinct from the [[Gupta Empire\|Gupta Empire]].

They set up the [[Vallabhi University\|Vallabhi University]] which was built along the lines of the [[Nalanda University\|Nalanda University]] and was also often compared with the same.

### Decline and Dissolution by 783 AD
After the decline of the [[Pushyabhuti Dynasty\|Pushyabhuti Dynasty]] itself, the Maitrakas saw a decline in the amount of revenue they can generate since they were now completely dependent upon themselves and their local produce. Arab Naval Raids were weakening them and having weaker diplomatic relations with the states around them with no power to leverage, they could no longer sustain wars and naval assaults against the Arabic Caliphates led by the [[Ummayyad Caliphate\|Ummayyads]] & later the [[Abassid Caliphate\|Abassids]] especially after the [[Islamisation of Sindh\|Islamisation of Sindh]] at the North-Western Border.

Apart from these logical deductions from the events with a few legends & folklore around the Maitrakas, there is no real evidence of what exactly caused the downfall and eventual dissolution in 776 AD. By 783 AD, the dynasty was fully wiped out.
