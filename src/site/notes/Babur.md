# Babur
> Born as "Zahir ud-Din Mohammad", the great-great-great-grandson of [[Timur\|Timur]] from his mother's side. Babur was the governor of Ferghana and Samarkand in the [[Timurid Empire\|Timurid Empire]] from 1494 to 1497 before later becoming the founding monarch of the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Empire]] who reigned from 1526 to 1530.

He was the one who first defeated [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] at [[First Battle of Panipat (1526)\|First Battle of Panipat (1526)]] followed by defeating [[Medini Rai\|Medini Rai]] in the [[Battle of Kalinjar (1528)\|Battle of Kalinjar (1528)]] and then defeated the [[Bengal Sultanate\|Sultān of Bengal]], [[Nushrat Shah\|Nushrat Shah]] at the [[Battle of Ghaghra River (1529)\|Battle of Ghaghra River (1529)]] before dying next year. 

He is also claimed to have defeated [[Rana Sanga\|Rana Sanga]] at [[Battle of Khanwa (1527)\|Battle of Khanwa (1527)]] but that is heavily disputed since Sanga did not die right there (he was injured) and died in Khanwa 8 months after the battle meaning he was there for 8 months i.e., Mughals lost on the 1st invasion & only won the second time around after Sanga had passed away. 

### Sandwiched in Kabul & invitation to India
Although originally the ruler of Ferdhana Valley, he was pushed backwards into Samarkanda after a defeat at the hands of his uncle [[Ulugh Beg II\|Ulugh Beg II]]. Even after his uncle passed away, he was pushed back to Bukhara & finally Kabul by [[Mohammad Shaybani\|Mohammad Shaybani]].

This is when [[Rana Sanga\|Rana Sanga]] is said to have contacted Babur since Rana knew Babur had new technology like [[Early Firearms\|Early Firearms]] and [[Cannon Artillery\|Cannon Artillery]]. When Rana realised that Babur might not hand him the keys to [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] and leave peacefully, Rana dropped his original plan.

In 1519 AD, he was also invited to invade the [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]]-led [[Lodhi Dynasty\|Lodhi Dynasty]] ruling the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] as the sultān. Invited by who? The serving governor of Punjab [[Daulat Khan Lodhi\|Daulat Khan Lodhi]] who was also the uncle of [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] - this began the [[First Battle of Panipat (1526)\|First Battle of Panipat (1526)]].

### Damage to Hindu Culture
He spent most of his reign warring with the other kingdoms around his own & had very little time to himself. It can be said that his major contributions include starting the construction of the [[Babri Masjid\|Babri Masjid]] in his name on top of the [[Old Ayodhya Temple\|Old Ayodhya Temple]], although some believe it is named after his male slave Babri with whom he is said to have had a gay relationship.

### Autobiography, Death and Children
He wrote his court biography himself (so he could have easily made up as he went).
His autobiography was named "Tuzuk-i-Babri" and translated into Persian as [[Bābur Nāmā\|Bābur Nāmā]] since Persian was the official language of the court in the Mughal Empire.

At the time of his death in 1530 AD, he followed the same tradition followed by [[Timur\|Timur]] in the [[Timurid Empire\|Timurid Empire]] to divide the Empire among the children in a way that even if one is the Amir or Emperor then the others . In the same fashion he divided the kingdom among his children. The problem was that Babur married many times and hence had many children from multiple wives. An arrangement was eventually agreed upon before his death.

[[Humayun\|Humayun]], his eldest child was made the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Emperor]].
[[Kamran Mirza\|Kamran Mirza]] was made the governor of Kabul and Kandahar.
[[Mohammad Askari Mirza\|Mohammad Askari Mirza]] was Kamran's male sibling who was the governor of Sambhal.
[[Abu Nasir Mohammad Hindal Mirza\|Abu Nasir Mohammad Hindal Mirza]] held governorship of Alwar, Mewat, Malwa and Ghazni.
[[Gulbadan Begum\|Gulbadan Begum]] was Babur's daughter who later wrote the [[Humāyūn Nāmā\|Humāyūn Nāmā]].
[[Gulchehra Begum\|Gulchehra Begum]] died while arriving to India in 1557 at the age of 41.
"Fakhr-un-Nissa" was the true first-born of Babur but she died as an infant.
