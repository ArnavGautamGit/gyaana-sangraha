# Vaishnava Tradition of Hinduism
> One of the largest sects of [[Hinduism\|Hinduism]] where [[Lord Vishnu\|Lord Vishnu]] and his incarnations [[Lord Krishna\|Lord Krishna]] and [[Lord Rāma\|Lord Rāma]] are revered & worshipped.

Vaishnava, like other sects in Hinduism are a part of the [[Bhakti Mārga\|Bhakti Mārga]] of attaining God.
Other Mārga (paths/मार्ग) do exist but we talk about them later.

### The Four Root Vaishnava Tradition
Over time after many landmark events and philosophical debates, 4 primary schools within the Vaishnava Tradition have emerged. All the Vaishnava practices are connected to these 4 roots. 

Each Vaishnava root tradition has their own type of [[Tilaka\|Tilaka]] and also believes in one or the other school from [[Vedānta\|Vedānta]] Philosophy as the siddhānta (foundational doctrine) of beliefs over which all the Sampradāya within the root tradition must follow.

1. [[Shrī Vaishnava\|Shrī Vaishnava]] - this is the traditional root from which both [[Shrī Rāmānuja Sampradāya\|Rāmānuja]] and [[Shrī Rāmānanda Sampradāya\|Rāmānanda]] Sampradāya have branched outwards from.
2. [[Brahma Vaishnava\|Brahma Vaishnava]] - this is the traditional root from which many popular vaishnava sampradāya like the [[Gaudiya Vaishnava Sampradāya\|Gaudiya Vaishnava]] and the [[Haridāsa Sampradāya\|Haridāsa Sampradāya]] have branched.
3. [[Rudra Vaishnava\|Rudra Vaishnava]] - this is the branch from which the [[Pushtimārga\|Pushtimārga]] Sampradāya.
4. [[Sankādi Vaishnava\|Sankādi Vaishnava]] - also called "Nimbarka" Vaishnava
