# Kanishka
> Known as Kanishka I of [[Kushan Empire\|Kushan Empire]] and also called Kanishka The Great, he was from Central Asian origin and famous in India for accepting [[Mahāyāna Buddhism\|Mahāyāna Buddhism]] and starting the [[Śaka Samvata Calendar\|Śaka Samvata Calendar]] in 78 AD.

The first king in RECORDED Indian History (having other sources than [[Literary Records\|Literary Records]]) to ever control North India & Central Asia at the same time.

### Controversy on who started the Śaka Samvata
Some hypothesise that he was not coronated in 78 AD but in 127 AD. In that thery, it was the [[Western Kshatrapa\|Western Kshatrapa]] (known as Indo-Scythians or Śaka or Shaka) tribes that started the calendar.

### Cultural Contributions
Kanishka is known to have contributed to [[Mathurā School of Buddhist Art\|Mathurā School of Buddhist Art]] along with the [[Gāndhāra School of Buddhist Art\|Gāndhāra School of Buddhist Art]] in his dual capitals of Mathura and Purushpura (Peshawar).

### Decline of Kushans after his Death
Later, the Scythians ended the Kushan Empire & the vacuum left in India helped the rise of the [[Gupta Empire\|Gupta Empire]]
