### Jizya in India across dynasties
During the times of the [[Khilji Dynasty\|Khilji Dynasty]] ruling the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]], the Jizya was charged as a part of the Kharaj Tax on income, revenue & agricultural produce. It was in these times where the Jizya was collected but meant to be subtle. 

When the [[Tughlaq Dynasty\|Tughlaq Dynasty]] began ruling the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]], it was [[Feroz Shah Tughlaq\|Feroz Shah Tughlaq]] who levied Jizya as a separate tax from the Kharaj Tax. This was done to show how he is a staunch islamist. It is said he did so to prove to the nobles he is more radical than others & try to avoid getting assassinated. In his definition, he levied it on everyone - including Brahmins who were excluded until now due to not being able to participate in economic activities. 

Due to this imposition, some practices in Hinduism became extractive, although individual's greed may also have been at play but now since they had to pay a tax, they had to earn & this greed for systematically incentivised. This is also the time when many Brahmins began to look towards agriculture to sustain their families & sell the surplus (to gain money to pay for Jizya).
