# Handle the Situation when your Boss is trying to Push You Out
> There come certain situations where regardless of if you are actually in the wrong or not, you may feel like there is an intentional target on your back with your boss giving you an intentional headache to make you leave the project or the company.

Never react emotionally, never react with the words "I am being targeted" or "I feel XYZ". As stated previously in [[Office Politics & How to Handle it\|Office Politics & How to Handle it]], You are suppose to document the lack of evidence provided by the management to build your case of the management being unfair. Documenting Evidence is completely distinct from emailing a journal entry.

If it is not an email conversation & they are doing it in person then follow the subheading "Can we have a quick chat?" in [[Office Politics & How to Handle it\|Office Politics & How to Handle it]] or just you say the following:

> [!cite] Ask for a written follow up in email.
> "I would like to document this conversation in writing. Would you please follow up by email?"

Either they would agree or ask you why you wish to document this meeting, in case they ask why you ask these questions in person as well as in email to the person you had this meeting with:

> [!cite] The Biggest Question (and often the only one needed)
> Could you please help me understand (in writing) the specific performance standards that I am not currently meeting?

If they ask why you want to know just say "I would like to have our standards documented so we are alligned going forward."

### Our Objective from asking this question?
Either they are being unfair about targeting you or there is some merit to their argument that you have missed a few performance standards they expected. 

In either case, you do not get fired as if they have some performance standards that you did not live up to, with these emails you at least showcase yourself to actually try and be better instead of just being desperate to keep your job and not be fired. Else, when they are firing you for no reason and you have the necessary documentation (screenshots of emails) you can easily present this document in court for a lawsuit against the company unless your are in the USA where it is "[[Employment At Will\|Employment At Will]]" and they can fire you legally without needing a viable reason.
