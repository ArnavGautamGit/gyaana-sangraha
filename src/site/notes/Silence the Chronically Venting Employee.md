# Silence the Chronically Venting Employee
> Venting once a while and complaining is okay unless you are doing it 24x7.
> This guide also works for chronically GOSSIPING employees

There is one guy at every office whose entire job seems to be to chronically vent about something or the other that is not orderly in the workplace. There are easy ways to identify them. While Venting is completely fine anywhere as long as 2 or more of these are not true:
1. They constantly complain but never fix the problem
2. Their venting drains your energy
3. Their venting ruins your focus.

If you have a certain individual with these qualities in your workplace who troubles you, you can easily deal with them on 4 different levels with each level increasing in intensity.

### Level 1: The Solution Check
When they interrupt your day with their rant of how nothing is working or good enough in the company and the people, you can simply cut them off and ask them if they have solutions:

> [!cite] They don't want solutions but an audience...
> YOU: "That sounds incredibly frustrating, what is your strategy to fix it?"
> THEM: *frozen with no response or giving a no-response*

### Level 2: The Boundary Check
When they keep coming, tell them the following:

> [!cite] I am at full capacity and can't take no more
> YOU: "I would love to help you vent about these issues but I am currently at capacitiy with my deliverables for today. We can circle back when I have more bandwidth."

### Level 3: The Silent Mirror
Let's say that they have not learnt any lessons and show up again on the second day, this time let them finish and hold eye contact for 3 seconds before dropping the following lines:

> [!cite] Show absolute Disinterest
> YOU: *hold eye contact for 3 seconds in absolute silence* 
> YOU: "Anyway... back to work."

### Level 4: The Nuclear Bomb Drop
We don't actually nuke them but this is nothing short of the same.
Depending upon how much you hate the person, you can choose to interrupt them or at least let them finish before you go ahead and say the following lines:

> [!cite] If its so bad... why're you here?
> YOU: "This seems like extremely unhappy in this role. Have you considered updating your Resume?"

Tell them directly that they have the power to leave the company and take their talents elsewhere. Joining the [[Dementors\|Dementors]] and [[Lord Voldemprt\|Lord Voldemprt]] is not a bad idea LMAO.
