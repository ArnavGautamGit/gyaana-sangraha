# Imperial Chola Dynasty
> The [[Sangam Era & Literature\|Sangam Era]] [[Chola Dynasty\|Chola Dynasty]] became Imperial as the medieval age began. They began to conquer more land with an organised army at the beginning of the 9th and 10th Century...

Imperial Cholas were widely regarded as "Great Temple Builders" in [[Indian History\|Indian History]] and are often a treated as a mark of pride for modern Tamils.

### Brief History & Major Emperors
The Imperialist Dynasty began with the king [[Rajaraja I Chola\|Rājarāja Chola]], also called [[Rājarāja I Chola\|Rajaraja I]] in 985 AD. 
It is belived that he was the "founder" of the Imperialist dynasty even though Cholas have been here since the [[Sangam Era & Literature\|Sangam Era]]. 

In Rājarāja Chola's reign, he defeated the [[Chera Dynasty\|Chera Dynasty]] at present-day Thiruvanthapuram and the [[Pandya Dynasty\|Pandya Dynasty]] in present-day northern Tamil Nadu. Rajaraja even defeated and captured the Maldives and northern Sri Lanka. He built temples to commemorate his military victories and conquests. Biggest example is the [[Brihadisavara Temple\|Brihadisavara Temple]]. His reign lasted until 1014 AD (29 years long reign) and after him, his son [[Rajendra I Chola\|Rājendra I]] succeeded him. It is said that Rajaraja moved the Capital from Vijayalaya to Thanjavur before passing away.

When [[Rajendra I Chola\|Rājendra]] became the emperor, he conquered the rest of Sri Lanka & wipes the Pandyas and Cheras completely... He defeats the Sri Vijaya empire and vassalises them. He establishes a trade relationship with China and wants to establish a halting station in Bengali ports (which is the region controlled by the [[Pala Dynasty of Bengal\|Pala Dynasty of Bengal]]) and refusal from [[Mahipala\|Mahipala]] causes their kingdom to fall completely. Due to controlling the Ganga Delta, he is called "Gangaikonda" i.e., conqueror of Ganga (as if he captured the entire [[North Indian Plains\|Gangetic Plains]] lol)

Rājendra I then decides to commemorate this victory by patronising the construction of Gangaikonda Cholapuram town. After Rājendra I passes away in 1044 AD, the succeeding rulers are weak and could not do much, eventually it brings a resurgence of [[Later Pandya Dynasty\|Later Pandyas]] and [[Later Chera Dynasty\|Later Cheras]].

### Administration
Administration was centralised where the king enjoyed full powers and honour with some slight elements of decentralisation. The official Royal Coronation ceremony was called as the "Vīrābhiśeka" i.e., Coronation of the Brave.

The Entire Empire was divided into smaller and smaller units which were called "Mandalam" followed by "Vālānādu" which comprised of many smaller units with each called "Nādu". 
A Nādu comprised of multiple villages with each village referred to as: "kurram".

Just like in the northern part of India, there were three kinds of villages, two of which are the same with the same names: Ura (for all) and Sabha (for Brahmins only) along with Economic cities and towns (soon to become cities) being called as "Nagaram".

Village-level self government also played a major role in maintaining peace and order, the criteria were mentioned in [[Uttarameru Inscriptions\|Uttarameru Inscriptions]] wirtten by [[Parantaka I\|Parantaka I]]. To apply for the position, a person must be between 35 to 70 years of age with more than 1.5 acre land and must know both [[Tamil (Language)\|Tamil]] & [[Sanskrit\|Sanskrit]] fluently.

### Cultural Contributions
Cholas built many temples, especially temples such as the [[Brihadisavara Temple\|Brihadisavara Temple]] and [[Gangaikonda Cholapuram Temple\|Gangaikonda Cholapuram Temple]]. The "Dravida" style of [[Hindu Temple Architecture\|Hindu Temple Architecture]] was popularised by them & this architecture has its own little subset named after the Cholas which was unique to them.

They exported spices, Ivory Tusks and other items such as statues, statuettes and other such items of art which reflected our culture and cuisine and gave [[Roman Bullions (Gold Coins)\|Roman Bullions (Gold Coins)]] in return as a form of payment. 

### Decline & Aftermath
As the source of gold from the Romans dried up due to the [[Holy Roman Empire\|Holy Roman Empire]] becoming weaker and flow of gold becoming sparser, it led to a decline in economy and an agrarian economy could not sustain that massive empire... causing its collapse.

Cholas left a sudden power vacuum in the regions they disappeared from, in Bengal there was a rise of the [[Sena Dynasty\|Sena Dynasty]] which would quickly be replaced by the [[Deva Dynasty\|Deva Dynasty]] in about 1-2 centuries.

Their deccan territories got taken over by other empires or went independent. They got reduced to right where they were in the [[Sangam Era & Literature\|Sangam Era]] as [[Later Pandya Dynasty\|Later Pandyas]] and [[Later Chera Dynasty\|Later Cheras]] returned along with the rise of [[Later Pallava Dynasty\|Later Pallava Dynasty]].
