# First Battle of Panipat (1526)
> Battle fought in 1526 AD by the [[Lodhi Dynasty\|Lodhi Dynasty]] of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] led by [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] against the invading force of [[Mughal Empire\|Mughals]] led by [[Babur\|Babur]].

While [[Babur\|Babur]]'s forces carry the [[Cannon Artillery\|Cannon Artillery]] and [[Early Firearms\|Early Firearms]], the defending force led by [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] did not have such technology and relied on techniques and military tactics of the bygone pre-gunpowder era that have been rendered useless by Cannons and early guns.

### Prologue to the Battle
After Babur's uncle [[Ulugh Beg II\|Ulugh Beg II]] had passed away, Babur had been getting his ass handed to him by the new heir [[Mohammad Shaybani\|Mohammad Shaybani]] who was driving him out of Ghazni and then down to Kabul.

At Kabul, he had first received the invitation (allegedly) from [[Rana Sanga\|Rana Sanga]] to attack the [[Lodhi Dynasty\|Lodhis]] of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] so that Rana could sit on the throne of Delhi. However when Rana realised that Babur may not leave once he gets here, the plan was dropped. 

In 1519 AD, [[Daulat Khan Lodhi\|Daulat Khan Lodhi]] (the uncle of [[Ibrahim Lodhi\|Ibrahim Lodhi]] and then Governor of Punjab) writes a letter to [[Babur\|Babur]] detailing that he might defect to support the Mughals if Babur were to attack as Ibrahim is already struggling in an internal power struggle amongst his relatives who all want power.

### The Shock & The Skirmish - Lahore
In 1524 AD, by the time [[Babur\|Babur]] almost reaches Lahore, he finds that Ibrahim's army has deposed [[Daulat Khan Lodhi\|Daulat Khan Lodhi]] from his position of Governor and now the small army that was stationed in Lahore to see that Daulat is deposed & cannot rebel is going to attack them. As the invader becomes the invaded, this small militia meant to maintain law & order was killed by Babur's much larger and much more technologically advanced army.

By 1525 AD, [[Babur\|Babur]]'s forces (while Babur was hiding on the other side of the Indus) had secured Punjab till the Chenab river (Chenab is the fifth & last river of Punjab) and were ready to cross towards the city of Delhi. Lodhi's larger army had left Delhi to attack the invaders & [[Babur\|Babur]] was now expecting such surprises after what happened in Lahore

### The Battle at Panipat
As the invading forces advanced towards Delhi and Delhi's forces moved towards Chenab river front, they met each other at Panipat.

Similar to how the Czech Legend [[Jan Žizka\|Jan Žizka]] used Wagons in the battles he fought in the 1400s, Babur decided to create a line of 700 wagons - each mounted and defended by [[Musket\|Musketeers]] and [[Cannon Artillery\|Cannon Artillery]] to target enemy cavalry. This line was defended by regular troops and their own Mughal Cavalry.

The Muskets and Cannons pinned the centre of Ibrahim's forces while cavalry and regular troops made sure that the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] cannot flank them. It was trouble for Sultān as eventually Ibrahim's forces were getting trampled by their own forces as the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal]] Cannons produced very loud sounds which scared the defending elephants into trampling friendlies around them.

The result was an easy victory for [[Babur\|Babur]]'s forces who later that year setup a [[Mughal Empire\|Mughal Empire]] distinct of the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]] that was ruled from Agra instead of Delhi. Agra at the time was the new city founded by [[Sikandar Lodhi\|Sikandar Lodhi]] in 1504 AD.


### Aftermath
[[Babur\|Babur]] who was emboldened by this war effort still had to fight the legendary [[Rana Sanga\|Rana Sanga]] at the [[Battle of Khanwa (1527)\|Battle of Khanwa (1527)]] which was a stalemate at the least or victory for Rana Sanga (despite what the degenerate Mughal paid propagandists wrote) because Rana stayed in Khanwa for 8 more months while he was being treated until he passed away of old age & worsening health.

It was only after Rana passed away that Mughals gained control of the region.
It was only after these pivotal battles that the [[Mughal Empire\|Mughals]] were able to control North India in peace.
