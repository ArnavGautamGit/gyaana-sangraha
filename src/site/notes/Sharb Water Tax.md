# Sharb Water Tax
> A tax on agricultural irrigation water provided by the state via state-built irrigation canals. Unlike modern times, it was not included in the tax on agriculture produce or any other general income tax but this was made to recover costs for the construction.

As mentioned before, it was charged separately from income tax the same way [[Jizya\|Jizya]] was a lot of the times but it was charged on everyone whose lands were being irrigated equally (regardless of religion) which means it was slightly different as well in that regard. 

It was first begun by [[Feroz Shah Tughlaq\|Feroz Shah Tughlaq]], the 17th [[Delhi Sultanate\|Sultān of Delhi]] from the [[Tughlaq Dynasty\|Tughlaq Dynasty]].
I will update these facts if I am proven wrong or if this tax is older than his reign (from 1351 to 1388 AD) which would mean that the tax was going on since before him & he did not start it. For now, I am fairly sure that he did.
