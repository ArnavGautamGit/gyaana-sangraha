# Command Respect at the Workplace
> Everyone wants a basic amount of respect at the workplace, this guide is not a how-to on making your boss perform a [[Sajda\|Sajda]] and [[Paibos\|Paibos]] everytime he sees you but it will get you the respect you need.

Your Boss or any other colleague just told you to SHUT UP & SIT DOWN in a loud tone at the meeting for asking questions about a confusing directive? If you turn into Captain Crashout & you lash out at your boss about this, then you probably took it too far but if you sit back and take it, they are empowered to do it again. 

What do you do then? Follow this guide which tells you 3 quick levels to professionally check them without getting fired, ofcourse. (Level 3 is savage)

### Level 1: The Clinical Clarification
They make an out-of-line-comment, you completely remove the emotions.
You ask them whether you should not understand the specifics of the directive.
Expose their idiocy in front of the entire room.

> [!cite] Logic Over Emotions
> "I fully understand this directive. Are you saying we should not try to understand what you are telling us to do?"

This exposes how stupif their argument sounds to everyone else in the room.Let the room get uncomfortable.

### Level 2: The Protocol Trap
Use the same professional protocols that HR uses to trap them into their own words right then and there in front of the entire room.

Stare at them for 3 full seconds and let the room get uncomfortable before saying the following:

 > [!cite] The Professional Slap that echoes forever
 > "I am fully committed to executing on our targets, but I cannot operate effectively undewr this kind of a conversation style. Was my question out-of-line? Or is this how you normally respond to team members that have concerns?"

### Level 3: The Paper Trail
You do not engage with this at the time but send an email after the meeting which sets a paper trail that you can send to the HR at the drop of a dime.

> [!cite] Email to Boss:
> "Boss,
> Regarding your comments directed at me in this morning's meeting, I want to ensure my work environment remains productive. Moving Forward, I will submit all questions or concerns strictly via email. Let me know if I should reach out to anyone else for clarity on new initiatives."

Next time, this happens, it will happen on a written email trail which can be sent to the HR at the drop of the dime and maybe get your boss/manager fired if he is anything below C-Suite or potentially evolve into a Workplace Harrassment Case that you will win due to having all prior evidences.
