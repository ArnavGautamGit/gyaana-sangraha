# Mamluk Dynasty (Delhi)
> A dynasty of [[Shia Muslims\|Shia Muslims]] ruled by [[Mohammad Ghori\|Mohammad Ghori]]'s former [[Mamluk\|Mamluk]] slave and general [[Qutub ud-Din Aibak\|Qutub ud-Din Aibak]] who ruled from Delhi and founded the [[Delhi Sultanate\|Delhi Sultanate]]. 
> 
> No other ruler after Aibak himself was Shia, all other Sultāns starting from [[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Iltutmish]] were [[Sunni Muslims\|Sunni Muslims]].

The first ruler was [[Qutub ud-Din Aibak\|Qutub ud-Din Aibak]] who ruled from 1206-1211. His biggest achievement was smashing 27 Hindu and Jain Temples to pieces and using those remains to construct the [[Qutub Minar\|Qutub Minar]], or at least begin its construction. The source of this information about the Qutub Minar is the signboard erected there by the [[Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)\|Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)]].

Qutub also started (and finished) the contsruction of [[Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque\|Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque]] whose name translates to "Might of Islam Mosque". I guess the real mightly are those who predate them and still exist after all of the Mamluks died i.e., as of now, Hindus had the last laugh.

The Qutub Minar complex is also home to the tombs of Qutub and his successors starting from "[[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Shams ud-Din Iltutmish]]" among possibly other Sultāns who completed and expanded upon it since Qutub was only able to get the ground floor of the [[Qutub Minar\|Qutub Minar]] built in his entire reign before dying.

### Sequence of Sultāns
1. [[Qutub ud-Din Aibak\|Qutub ud-Din Aibak]] ---> 1206 AD to 1211 AD
2. [[Aram Shah\|Aram Shah]] ---> 1210 AD to 1211 AD 
	1. Oversaw the rise of [[Udaysimha\|Udaysimha]] of [[Chahamanas of Jalor\|Jalor Chauhans]])
3. [[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Shams ud-Din Iltutmish]] ---> 1211 AD to 1236 AD 
	1. [[Mamluk\|Mamluk]] of [[Qutub ud-Din Aibak\|Qutub ud-Din Aibak]].
	2. Fathered 1 daughter and 4 younger sons from separate wives who all became Sultāns
	3. None of his children were strong rulers
4. [[Rukn ud-Din Firuz\|Rukn ud-Din Firuz]] (or "Firuz I") ---> 1236 AD 
	1. Second-born Son (third child overall) of [[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Shams ud-Din Iltutmish]]
	2. Only Ruled for 7 months since coronation as Sultān.
5. [[Razia Sultān\|Razia Sultān]] (or "Razia Sultānā") ---> 1236 AD to 1240 AD
	1. The first female ruler of Muslim ruler in the Indian Subcontinent
	2. The first female Sultān of Delhi 
	3. Eldest Daughter and Child of [[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Shams ud-Din Iltutmish]].
	4. Regnal Name: "Jalālat ud-Din Razia"
	5. She was disposed in 1240, married the rebel leader and waged war for kingdom but lost to her half-brother [[Muizzuddin Bahram\|Muizzuddin Bahram]]
	6. Assassinated by [[Corps of 40\|Corps of 40]] soon after.
6. [[Muizzuddin Bahram\|Muizzuddin Bahram]] ---> 1240 AD to 1242 AD
	1. Fourth Child of [[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Shams ud-Din Iltutmish]] and Third brother of [[Razia Sultān\|Razia Sultān]].
	2. Muizzuddin defeated his half-sister to retain the kingdom
	3. Was remote controlled by the [[Corps of 40\|Corps of 40]] until they assassinated him.
7. [[Alauddin Masud\|Alauddin Masud]] ---> 1242 AD to 1246 AD
	1. Youngest Child of [[Shams ud-Din Iltutmish\|Shams ud-Din Iltutmish]] and fourth brother of [[Razia Sultān\|Razia Sultān]].
	2. [[Tughra Khan\|Tughra Khan]], the governor of Bengal, broke away and declared Independence.
	3. Tughra Khan, [[Bengal Sultanate\|Sultān of Bengal]] won Bihar and even threatened Awadh.
	4. [[Nasiruddin Mahmud\|Nasiruddin Mahmud]], his mother and [[Ghiyas ud-Din Balban\|Ghiyas ud-Din Balban]] conspired against him.
	5. Deposed from the throne (by assassination) at the age of 14 or 19 in 1246 AD.
8. [[Nasiruddin Mahmud\|Nasiruddin Mahmud]] ---> 1246 AD to 1266 AD
	1. Appointed [[Ghiyas ud-Din Balban\|Ghiyas ud-Din Balban]] as his Nāib Sultān (Deputy Sultān)
9. [[Ghiyas ud-Din Balban\|Ghiyas ud-Din Balban]] ---> 1266 AD to 1287 AD
10. [[Muizzuddin Qaiqabad\|Muizzuddin Qaiqabad]] ---> 1287 AD to 1290 AD
