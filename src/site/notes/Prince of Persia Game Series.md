# Prince of Persia Game Series
> This is one of Ubisoft's best work alongside the early part of [[Assasin's Creed Game Series\|Assasin's Creed Game Series]] and [[Far Cry Game Series\|Far Cry Game Series]] that brought them where they are today. Some lesser known Ubisoft games like [[Just Dance Game Series\|Just Dance Game Series]] and [[Tom Clancy Game Series\|Tom Clancy Game Series]].

Before a certain time, this series was made by Broderlands company, it is unclear whether it was purchased by Ubisoft or turned into Ubisoft or just the Prince of Persia IP was purchased from them but Ubisoft rebooted the series into a coherent and connected main storyline.

The First game in the original trilogy was [[Prince of Persia - The Sands of Time\|Prince of Persia - The Sands of Time]] which was released in 2003 for the PS2 and later for PS3 and whatever Xbox equivalent. 

The next game was [[Prince of Persia - Warrior Within\|Prince of Persia - Warrior Within]], released on the same platforms in 2004.
The game [[Prince of Persia - The Two Thrones\|Prince of Persia - The Two Thrones]] wrapped up the trilogy in 2005 released for the same platforms.

Also in 2005, when the [[PlayStation Portable (PSP)\|PSP]] was released, [[Prince of Persia - Warrior Within\|Prince of Persia - Warrior Within]] was remastered for the PSP and renamed as [[Prince of Persia - Revelations\|Prince of Persia - Revelations]]. 

Similarly, in 2006 the [[Prince of Persia - The Two Thrones\|Prince of Persia - The Two Thrones]] was remastered by Pipeworks Software for the PSP and renamed as [[Prince of Persia - Rival Swords\|Prince of Persia - Rival Swords]].

Later Ubisoft tried to set up a new alternate universe for the new games, potentially as a new trilogy with the game [[Prince of Persia - Forgotten Sands\|Prince of Persia - Forgotten Sands]] with platformer-like acrobatics which was supposed to be a throwback to pre-Ubisoft roots of the game. Forgotten Sands ended up being so hated by 2007 and [[Assassin's Creed (2007 Game)\|Assassin's Creed (2007 Game)]] was so loved that this game put the Prince of Persia series in an indefinite hiatus.
